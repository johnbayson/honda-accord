Troubleshoot the Transmission Control Module (TCM) Circuit
NOTE: Do this troubleshooting procedure when the following symptoms occur at the same time.
  • The MIL comes on (DTC U0047 in the fuel and emissions system comes on).
  • The A/T gear position indicator does not work.
  • Poor acceleration when the vehicle starting off.
  • The transmission does not shift (stuck on 4th gear).
  • The vehicle does not move in R.
  1. Check the No. 10 (10 A) fuse in the driver's under-dash fuse/relay box.
Is the fuse OK?
YES -
Reinstall the checked fuse, then go to Step 2 .
NO -
Check for short to body ground in the wire between TCM connector No. 5 and the driver's under-dash fuse/relay box, and repair it if necessary. Also replace the No. 10 (10 A) fuse in the driver's under-dash fuse/relay box, then recheck.■
  1. Check the No. 17 (20 A) fuse in the under-hood fuse/relay box.
Is the fuse OK?
YES -
Reinstall the checked fuse, then go to Step 3 .
NO -
Check for short to body ground in the wire between the TCM relay 4P connector and the under-hood fuse/relay box, and repair it if necessary. Also replace the No. 17 (20 A) fuse in the under-hood fuse/relay box, then recheck.■
  1. Remove the TCM relay.
  1. Test the TCM relay.
Is the TCM relay OK?
YES -
Go to Step 5 .
NO -
Replace the TCM relay .■
  1. Measure the voltage between TCM relay 4P connector terminals No. 3 and No. 4 when the ignition switch is turned to ON (II), and when the ignition switch is turned to LOCK (0).
Is there about battery voltage when the ignition switch is turned to ON (II), and about 0 V when the ignition switch is turned to LOCK (0)?
YES -
Go to Step 6 .
NO -
Go to Step 10 .
  1. Disconnect the TCM connector.
  1. Connect TCM relay 4P connector terminals No. 1 and No. 2 with a jumper wire.
  1. Measure the voltage between TCM connector terminal No. 6 and body ground.
Is there about battery voltage?
YES -
Go to Step 14 .
NO -
Go to Step 9 .
  1. Measure the voltage between TCM relay 4P connector terminal No. 2 and body ground.
Is there about battery voltage?
YES -
Repair open in the wire between TCM connector terminal No. 6 and the driver's under-dash fuse/relay box.■
NO -
Repair open in the wire between TCM relay 4P connector terminal No. 2 and the under-hood fuse/relay box.■
  1. Measure the voltage between TCM relay 4P connector terminal No. 4 and body ground.
Is there about battery voltage?
YES -
Go to Step 11 .
NO -
Repair open in the wire between TCM relay 4P connector terminal No. 4 and the under-hood fuse/relay box.■
  1. Install the TCM relay.
  1. Disconnect the TCM connector.
  1. Measure the voltage between TCM connector terminal No. 7 and body ground.
Is there about battery voltage?
YES -
Replace the TCM. ■
NO -
Repair open in the wire between TCM connector terminal No. 7 and the TCM relay 4P connector.■
  1. Turn the ignition switch to ON (II).
  1. Measure the voltage between TCM connector terminal No. 5 and body ground.
Is there about battery voltage?
YES -
Go to Step 16 .
NO -
Repair open in the wire between TCM connector terminal No. 5 and the TCM relay connector.■
  1. Check for continuity between TCM connector terminal No. 52, No. 59 and body ground.
Is there continuity?
YES -
Go to Step 17 .
NO -
Repair open in the wire between TCM connector terminal No. 52, No. 59 and body ground (G151).■
  1. Disconnect the output shaft (countershaft) speed sensor connector.
  1. Check for continuity between TCM connector terminal No. 30 and body ground.
Is there continuity?
YES -
Repair short to body ground in the wire between TCM connector terminal No. 30 and the output shaft (countershaft) speed sensor connector.■
NO -
Go to Step 19 .
  1. Disconnect the input shaft (mainshaft) speed sensor connector.
  1. Check for continuity between TCM connector terminal No. 18 and body ground.
Is there continuity?
YES -
Replace the TCM. ■
NO -
Repair short to body ground in the wire between TCM connector terminal No. 18 and the input shaft (mainshaft) speed sensor connector.■
