DTC Troubleshooting: B2980 (K and AUTO)
DTC B2980 or DTC indicator K and AUTO:
 A Short in the A/C Pressure Sensor Circuit
  1. Clear the DTC with the HDS.
  1. Turn the ignition switch to LOCK (0), and then to ON (II).
  1. Do the self-diagnostic with the HDS or climate control unit.
  1. Check for DTCs.
Is DTC B2980 or K and AUTO indicated?
YES -
Go to Step 5 .
NO -
Intermittent failure.■
  1. Turn the ignition switch to LOCK (0).
  1. Disconnect the A/C pressure sensor 3P connector.
  1. Disconnect climate control unit connector B (12P).
  1. Check for continuity between body ground and climate control unit connector B (12P) No. 11 and No. 12 terminals individually.
Is there continuity?
YES -
Repair short to body ground in the wire(s) between the climate control unit and the A/C pressure sensor.■
NO -
Go to Step 9 .
  1. Check for continuity between climate control unit connector B (12P) No. 10 terminal and climate control unit connector B (12P) No. 11 and No. 12 terminals individually.
Is there continuity?
YES -
Repair short in the wires.■
NO -
Go to Step 10 .
  1. Check for continuity between climate control unit connector B (12P) No. 12 terminal and the A/C pressure sensor 3P connector No. 1 terminal.
Is there continuity?
YES -
Go to Step 11 .
NO -
Repair open in the wire between the climate control unit and the A/C pressure sensor.■
  1. Reconnect climate control unit connector B (12P).
  1. Turn the ignition switch to ON (II).
  1. Measure the voltage between climate control unit connector B (12P) No. 10 terminal and climate control unit connector B (12P) No. 11 and No. 12 terminals individually.
Is there about 5 V?
YES -
Replace the A/C pressure sensor.■
NO -
Substitute a known-good climate control unit, and recheck. If the symptom/indication goes away, replace the original climate control unit .■
