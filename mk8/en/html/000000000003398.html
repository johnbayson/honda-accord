Interior Trim Removal/Installation - Pillar Areas
Quarter Pillar Trim
SRS components are located in this area. Review the SRS component locations and the precautions and procedures before doing repairs or service.
NOTE:
  • Put on gloves to protect your hands.
  • Take care not to scratch the trim and the panels.
  • When removing components, use the trim tools or equivalent.
  1. Remove the tailgate weatherstrip.
  1. Detach the hooks (A) and tabs (B) from the quarter pillar trim (C) on either side, and pull the roof end trim (D) down by hand to detach the clips (E, F), then remove it.
  1. Remove these items:
    • Tonneau cover and cargo net, for some models
    • Rear under floor cover:
      • -
      With temporary repair kit (TRK)
      -
      With compact spare tyre/with normal spare tyre
    • Rear seat cushion
    • Rear seat belt lower anchor
    • Rear seat side bolster
    • Damper maintenance lid
    • Cargo net striker, for some models
    • Rear seat belt anchor wire
    • Cargo area side trim panel
    • Rear door opening seal, as needed
  1. Remove the quarter pillar trim (A).
    -1.
    Pull the quarter pillar bottom of the trim back by hand to detach the clip (B) and the pin (C).
    -2.
    Pull the rear pillar bottom of the trim back by hand to detach the clips (D).
    -3.
    Pull the trim down to detach the clips (E), and to release the upper hooks (F, G) from the headliner (H) and the upper hook (I) from a body hole.
  1. Install the trim in the reverse order of removal, and note these items:
    • If the clips (A) are damaged or stress-whitened, replace them with new ones.
    • If the side curtain airbag has deployed, replace the quarter pillar trim with a new one.
    • To prevent the side curtain airbags from deploying improperly and possibly causing injury, inspect the trim and replace it if it has any of these types of damage:
      • -
      Any cracks or deformations in the quarter pillar trim (B) and the upper hook (C), and any stress-whitened areas in the upper part of the trim
      -
      Any cracks, or stress-whitened areas in the clip seating surfaces (D)
    • Replace any damaged parts with new ones.
    • Make sure the top of the trim overlaps with the headliner correctly.
    • Make sure the trim hook is installed into the hole in the body securely.
    • Push the clips and the pin into place securely.
