Fuel Filter Water Bleeding
  1. Lift the vehicle, and support it with jackstands.
  1. Turn the steering wheel to the left full lock position.
  1. Loosen the drain plug (A) by a wrench.
  1. Loosen the drain plug until about 3−5 mm by a hand.
  1. Connect the hose (piping diameter 6 mm), and place a suitable container under the hose to catch up the water and/or fuel.
  1. Turn the ignition switch to ON (II), but do not operate the starter. After the presupply fuel pump runs for about 2 seconds, turn the ignition switch to OFF. Then check for contain the fuel in the container.
    NOTE: If lots of the water or contamination is contained, replace the fuel filter element, or clean inside of the fuel filter housing.
  1. Remove the drain plug.
  1. Coat the new drain plug O-ring with clean fuel, then install the drain plug with the O-ring.
  1. Wipe off the fuel on the floor, then lower the vehicle
  1. Turn the ignition switch to ON (II), wait 30 seconds, then check for fuel leakage.
  1. Start the engine, and then check the showing WATER IN FUEL FILTER (A) in the information display (B).
  • If the WATER IN FUEL FILTER display is on, go to Step 12 .
  • If the WATER IN FUEL FILTER display is not on, fuel filter water bleeding is complete.
  1. Disconnect the fuel filter water level switch 2P connector, and then recheck the WATER IN FUEL FILTER display.
    • If the WATER IN FUEL FILTER display is on, repair short in the LT BLU wire between the fuel filter water level switch and ECM connector No. 2 terminal No. 40. If wire is OK, substitute a known-good ECM, then recheck. If the symptom/indication goes away with a known-good ECM, replace the original ECM.
    • If the WATER IN FUEL FILTER display is not on, replace the fuel filter water level switch.
