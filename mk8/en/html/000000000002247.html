Fuel Filter Element Removal and Installation
Special Tools Required

Fuel filter wrench
 070AA-RL00200
Removal
  1. Remove the engine cover.
  1. Remove the strut brace.
  1. Remove the nut (A), then reposition the under-hood fuse/relay box (B).
  1. Lift the vehicle, and support it with jackstands.
  1. Turn the steering wheel to the left full lock position.
  1. Loosen the drain plug (A) by a wrench.
  1. Loosen the drain plug until about 3−5 mm by a hand.
  1. Connect the hose (piping diameter 6 mm), and place a suitable container under the hose to catch up the water and/or fuel.
  1. Remove the fuel feed hose joint (A).
    NOTE: When the fuel feed hose joint removed, fuel drain from the drain plug.
  1. Wipe off the fuel on the floor, then lower the vehicle.
  1. Attach the fuel filter wrench, then remove the cap (A).
  1. Remove the fuel filter element (B).
    NOTE: Clean inside of the fuel filter housing, if necessary.
Installation
  1. Install a new O-ring (A), and the new fuel filter element (B) to the fuel filter cap (C).
    NOTE: Coat the new O-ring with clean fuel.
  1. Insert the fuel cap. Using the special tool, tighten the cap to the special torque.
    NOTE: After tightening, make sure the marks (D) are still aligned.
  1. Coat the new drain plug O-ring with clean fuel, then install the drain plug (A) with the O-ring.
  1. Install the fuel feed hose joint with the new O-ring (B).
    NOTE:
    • Coat the new O-ring with clean fuel.
    • Use all the new parts supplied in the fuel filter replacement kit.
  1. Wipe off the fuel on the floor.
  1. Install the strut brace, and the under-hood fuse/relay box.
  1. Turn the ignition switch to ON (II), wait about 40 seconds, then check for fuel leakage.
  1. Start the engine.
  1. Install the engine cover.
