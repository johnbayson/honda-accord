Brake Pedal and Brake Pedal Position Switch Adjustment
Pedal Height
  1. Remove the driver's dashboard undercover.
  1. Turn the brake pedal position switch (A) counterclockwise, and pull it back until it is no longer touching the brake pedal.
  1. Remove the footrest, then remove the steering joint cover.
  1. Pull back the carpet and find the cutout (B) in the insulation. Measure the pedal height (C) at the center of the pedal pad (D) to the floor without the insulation.
    Standard pedal height (with carpet removed):
    LHD model:
    M/T:
    164 mm (6.46 in.)
    A/T:
    173 mm (6.81 in.)
    RHD model:
    M/T:
    167 mm (6.57 in.)
    A/T:
    172 mm (6.77 in.)

  1. Loosen the pushrod locknut (A), and screw the pushrod (B) in or out with pliers until the standard pedal height from the floor is reached. After adjustment, tighten the locknut firmly. Do not adjust the pedal height with the pushrod pressed.
Brake Pedal Position Switch Clearance
  1. Lift up on the brake pedal by hand. Push in the brake pedal position switch until its plunger is fully pressed (threaded end (A) touching the pad (B) on the pedal arm). Turn the switch 45 ° clockwise to lock it. The gap between the brake pedal position switch and the pad is automatically adjusted to 0.7 mm (0.028 in.) by locking the switch. Make sure the brake lights go off when the pedal is released.
  1. Install the all removed parts in the reverse order of removal.
  1. Check the brake pedal free play.
Pedal Free Play
  1. With the ignition switch in LOCK (0), inspect the play (A) at the brake pedal pad (B) by pushing the brake pedal by hand. If the brake pedal free play is out of specification, adjust the brake pedal position switch (C). If the brake pedal free play is insufficient, it may result in brake drag.
    Free play:
    1−5 mm (0.04−0.20 in.)
