DTC Troubleshooting: P0135 (41)
DTC P0135:
 A/F Sensor 1 Heater Circuit Malfunction
NOTE:
  • Before you troubleshoot, record all freeze data and review the general troubleshooting information.
  • If DTC P1036 or P1038 is stored at the same time as DTC P0135, troubleshoot DTC P1036 or P1038 first, then recheck for DTC P0135.
  1. Turn the ignition switch to ON (II).
  1. Clear the DTC with the HDS.
  1. Start the engine, and hold the engine speed at 2,500 rpm (min −1 ) without load (A/T in P or N, M/T in neutral) for 3 minutes, then let it idle.
  1. Check for Temporary DTCs or DTCs with the HDS.
Is DTC P0135 indicated?
YES -
Go to Step 5 .
NO -
Intermittent failure, the system is OK at this time. Check for poor connections or loose terminals at A/F sensor 1, the A/F sensor relay, and the ECM.■
  1. Turn the ignition switch to LOCK (0).
  1. Check the No. 14 LAF (A/F SENSOR) (10 A) fuse in the under-hood fuse/relay box.
Is the fuse OK?
YES -
Go to Step 7 .
NO -
Go to Step 16 .
  1. Remove the A/F sensor relay from the under-hood fuse/relay box.
  1. Test the A/F sensor relay.
Is the A/F sensor relay OK?
YES -
Go to Step 9 .
NO -
Replace the A/F sensor relay, then go to Step 20 .
  1. Disconnect the A/F sensor 1 6P connector.
  1. At A/F sensor 1 side, measure the resistance between A/F sensor 1 6P connector terminals No. 2 and No. 5.
Is there 2.6−4.0 Ω at 20 °C (68 °F)?
YES -
Go to Step 11 .
NO -
Go to Step 19 .
  1. At A/F sensor 1 side, check for continuity between A/F sensor 1 6P connector terminals No. 5 and No. 1, No. 3, No. 4, and No. 6 individually.
Is there continuity?
YES -
Go to Step 19 .
NO -
Go to Step 12 .
  1. At A/F sensor 1 side, check for continuity between A/F sensor 1 6P connector terminals No. 2 and No. 1, No. 3, No. 4, and No. 6 individually.
Is there continuity?
YES -
Go to Step 19 .
NO -
Go to Step 13 .
  1. Disconnect ECM connector No. 1 (96P).
  1. Check for continuity between ECM connector No. 1 terminal No. 69 and A/F sensor 1 6P connector terminal No. 5.
Is there continuity?
YES -
Go to Step 15 .
NO -
Repair open in the wire between A/F sensor 1 and ECM connector No. 1 terminal No. 69, then go to Step 20 .
  1. Check for continuity between A/F sensor 1 6P connector terminal No. 2 and A/F sensor relay 4P connector terminal No. 2.
Is there continuity?
YES -
Check the under-hood fuse/relay box, and replace it if needed, then go to Step 20 . If the under-hood fuse/relay box is OK, go to Step 25 .
NO -
Repair open in the wire between A/F sensor 1 and the A/F sensor 1 relay, then go to Step 20 .
  1. Remove the A/F sensor relay from the under-hood fuse/relay box.
  1. Disconnect the A/F sensor 1 6P connector.
  1. Check for continuity between A/F sensor 1 6P connector terminal No. 2 and body ground.
Is there continuity?
YES -
Repair short in the wire between the A/F sensor relay and A/F sensor 1. Also replace the No. 14 LAF (A/F SENSOR) (10 A) fuse, then go to Step 20 .
NO -
Check the under-hood fuse/relay box, and replace it if needed, then go to Step 20 .
  1. Replace A/F sensor 1.
  1. Reconnect all connectors.
  1. Turn the ignition switch to ON (II).
  1. Reset the ECM with the HDS.
  1. Start the engine, and hold the engine speed at 2,500 rpm (min −1 ) without load (A/T in P or N, M/T in neutral) for 3 minutes, then let it idle.
  1. Check for Temporary DTCs or DTCs with the HDS.
Is DTC P0135 indicated?
YES -
Check for poor connections or loose terminals at A/F sensor 1, the A/F sensor relay, and the ECM, then go to Step 1 .
NO -
Troubleshooting is complete. If any other Temporary DTCs or DTCs are indicated, go to the indicated DTC's troubleshooting.■
  1. Reconnect all connectors.
  1. Update the ECM if it does not have the latest software, or substitute a known-good ECM.
  1. Start the engine, and hold the engine speed at 2,500 rpm (min −1 ) without load (A/T in P or N, M/T in neutral) for 3 minutes, then let it idle.
  1. Check for Temporary DTCs or DTCs with the HDS.
Is DTC P0135 indicated?
YES -
Check for poor connections or loose terminals at A/F sensor 1, the A/F sensor relay, and the ECM. If the ECM was updated, substitute a known-good ECM, then recheck. If the ECM was substituted, go to step 1.
NO -
If the ECM was updated, troubleshooting is complete. If the ECM was substituted, replace the original ECM. If any other Temporary DTCs or DTCs are indicated, go to the indicated DTC's troubleshooting.■
