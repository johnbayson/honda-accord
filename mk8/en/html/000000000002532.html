DTC Troubleshooting: P0718 (15)
DTC P0718:
 Input Shaft (Mainshaft) Speed Sensor Intermittent Failure
NOTE:
  • Before you troubleshoot, record all freeze data and any on-board snapshot with the HDS, and review General Troubleshooting Information.
  • This code is caused by an electrical circuit problem and cannot be caused by a mechanical problem in the transmission.
  1. Turn the ignition switch to ON (II).
  1. Clear the DTC with the HDS.
  1. Test-drive the vehicle with the shift lever in D, and let the transmission shift through all five gears at speeds over 20 km/h (12 mph). Slow down and stop the wheels.
  1. Monitor the OBD STATUS for P0718 in the DTCs MENU with the HDS.
Does the HDS indicate FAILED?
YES -
Go to Step 5 .
NO -
If the HDS indicates PASSED, intermittent failure, the system is OK at this time. Check for poor connections or loose terminals between the input shaft (mainshaft) speed sensor and the TCM. If the HDS indicates NOT COMPLETED, go to Step 3 .
  1. Turn the ignition switch to LOCK (0).
  1. Disconnect the input shaft (mainshaft) speed sensor connector, and inspect the connector and connector terminals to be sure they are making good contact.
Are the input shaft (mainshaft) speed sensor connector terminals OK?
YES -
Go to Step 7 .
NO -
Repair the connector terminals, then go to Step 20 .
  1. Disconnect the TCM connector.
  1. Check for continuity between TCM connector terminals No. 52 and body ground, and between No. 59 and body ground.
Is there continuity?
YES -
Go to Step 9 .
NO -
Repair open in the wires between TCM connector terminals No. 52, No. 59 and body ground (G151), or repair poor body ground (G151), then go to Step 20 .
  1. Connect the TCM connector.
  1. Turn the ignition switch to ON (II).
  1. Measure the voltage between input shaft (mainshaft) speed sensor connector terminal No. 1 and body ground.
Is there about 5 V?
YES -
Go to Step 12 .
NO -
Go to Step 17 .
  1. Measure the voltage between input shaft (mainshaft) speed sensor connector terminal No. 2 and body ground.
Is there about 5 V?
YES -
Replace the input shaft (mainshaft) speed sensor, then go to Step 20 .
NO -
Go to Step 13 .
  1. Turn the ignition switch to LOCK (0).
  1. Disconnect the TCM connector.
  1. Check for continuity between input shaft (mainshaft) speed sensor connector terminal No. 2 and body ground.
Is there continuity?
YES -
Repair short to body ground in the wire between TCM connector terminal No. 46 and the input shaft (mainshaft) speed sensor connector, then go to Step 20 .
NO -
Go to Step 16 .
  1. Check for continuity between TCM connector terminal No. 46 and input shaft (mainshaft) speed sensor connector terminal No. 2.
Is there continuity?
YES -
Go to Step 26 .
NO -
Repair open in the wire between TCM connector terminal No. 46 and the input shaft (mainshaft) speed sensor, then go to Step 20 .
  1. Turn the ignition switch to LOCK (0).
  1. Disconnect the TCM connector.
  1. Check for continuity between TCM connector terminal No. 18 and input shaft (mainshaft) speed sensor connector terminal No. 1.
Is there continuity?
YES -
Go to Step 26 .
NO -
Repair open in the wire between TCM connector terminal No. 18 and the input shaft (mainshaft) speed sensor, then go to Step 20 .
  1. Reconnect all connectors.
  1. Turn the ignition switch to ON (II).
  1. Clear the DTC with the HDS.
  1. Test-drive the vehicle with the shift lever in D, and let the transmission shift through all five gears at speeds over 20 km/h (12 mph). Slow down and stop the wheels.
  1. Check for Temporary DTCs or DTCs with the HDS.
Is DTC P0718 indicated?
YES -
Check for poor connections or loose terminals between the input shaft (mainshaft) speed sensor and the TCM, then go to Step 1 .
NO -
Go to Step 25 .
  1. Monitor the OBD STATUS for P0718 in the DTCs MENU with the HDS.
Does the HDS indicate PASSED?
YES -
Troubleshooting is complete. If any other Temporary DTCs or DTCs were indicated in Step 24 , go to the indicated DTC's troubleshooting.■
NO -
If the HDS indicates FAILED, check for poor connections or loose terminals between the input shaft (mainshaft) speed sensor and the TCM, then go to Step 1 . If the HDS indicates NOT COMPLETED, go to Step 23 .
  1. Reconnect all connectors.
  1. Update the TCM if it does not have the latest software, or substitute a known-good TCM.
  1. Test-drive the vehicle with the shift lever in D, and let the transmission shift through all five gears at speeds over 20 km/h (12 mph). Slow down and stop the wheels.
  1. Check for Temporary DTCs or DTCs with the HDS.
Is DTC P0718 indicated?
YES -
Check for poor connections or loose terminals between the input shaft (mainshaft) speed sensor and the TCM. If the TCM was updated, substitute a known-good TCM, then go to Step 28 . If the TCM was substituted, go to Step 1 .
NO -
Go to Step 30 .
  1. Monitor the OBD STATUS for P0718 in the DTCs MENU with the HDS.
Does the HDS indicate PASSED?
YES -
If the TCM was updated, troubleshooting is complete. If the TCM was substituted, replace the original TCM. If any other Temporary DTCs or DTCs were indicated in Step 29 , go to the indicated DTC's troubleshooting.■
NO -
If the HDS indicates FAILED, check for poor connections or loose terminals between the input shaft (mainshaft) speed sensor and the TCM. If the TCM was updated, substitute a known-good TCM, then go to Step 28 . If the TCM was substituted, go to Step 1 . If the HDS indicates NOT COMPLETED, go to Step 28 .
