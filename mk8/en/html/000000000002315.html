M/T Reverse Lockout System Circuit Troubleshooting
NOTE:
  • Refer to the How to Troubleshooting circuits at the ECM connectors in the Fuel and Emission Systems section before troubleshooting this circuit.
  • When checking if the transmission will shift to reverse while the wheels are being driven forward, raise the vehicle on the lift, make sure it is securely supported, and allow the front wheels to rotate freely.
  • Do not release the clutch pedal if you can select the transmission into reverse gear while the wheels are driven forward.
  • When the vehicle on the lift, and you make the wheels drive, turn off the VSA system by the VSA OFF switch.
Driver can select the reverse gear when vehicle speed is 20 km/h (12 mph) or less
  1. Turn the ignition switch to LOCK (0).
  1. Check a No. 13 (15 A) fuse in the under-hood fuse/relay box.
Is the fuse OK?
YES -
Reinstall the fuse, then go to Step 3 .
NO -
Install the new No. 13 (15 A) fuse, and recheck with the ignition switch is turned to ON (II). If the fuse continues to blow, check for short to body ground in the wire between the No. 13 (15 A) fuse in the under-hood fuse/relay box and the reverse lockout solenoid.■
  1. Test-drive the vehicle.
  1. Check for fuel and emissions system's DTC with the HDS.
Are there any DTCs stored in the ECM?
YES -
Go to the indicated ECM DTC's troubleshooting. ■
NO -
Go to Step 5 .
  1. Turn the ignition switch to LOCK (0).
  1. Remove PGM-FI main relay 2 (SOL), and check it.
Is the relay OK?
YES -
Go to Step 7 .
NO -
Replace PGM-FI main relay 2 (SOL). ■
  1. Measure the voltage between PGM-FI main relay 2 (SOL) 4P connector terminal No. 1 and body ground.
Is there battery voltage?
YES -
Go to Step 8 .
NO -
Repair or replace open in the wire (included in the under-hood fuse/relay box internal circuit) between the No. 13 (15 A) fuse in the under-hood fuse/relay box and the PGM-FI main relay 2 (SOL).■
  1. Install PGM-FI main relay 2 (SOL).
  1. Disconnect the reverse lockout solenoid 2P connector.
  1. Turn the ignition switch to ON (II).
  1. Measure the voltage between reverse lockout solenoid 2P connector terminal No. 2 and body ground.
Is there battery voltage?
YES -
Go to Step 12 .
NO -
Repair open in the wire between the reverse lockout solenoid and PGM-FI main relay 2 (SOL).■
  1. Turn the ignition switch to LOCK (0), and wait 2 minutes.
  1. Disconnect ECM connector No. 1 (96P).
  1. Check for continuity between the reverse lockout solenoid 2P connector terminal No. 1 and ECM connector No. 1 (96P) terminal No. 13.
Is the continuity?
YES -
Go to Step 15 .
NO -
Repair open in the wire between the reverse lockout solenoid and the ECM.■
  1. Do the reverse lockout solenoid test.
Is the reverse lockout solenoid OK?
YES -
Check for loose terminals or poor connections at the ECM. If necessary, update the ECM if it does not have the latest software, or substitute a known-good ECM, then recheck. If the trouble goes away with a known-good ECM, replace the original ECM. ■
NO -
Repair or replace the reverse lockout solenoid. ■
