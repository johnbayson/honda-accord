DTC Troubleshooting: P0711 (28)
DTC P0711:
 Problem in ATF Temperature Sensor Circuit
NOTE:
  • Before you troubleshoot, record all freeze data and any on-board snapshot with the HDS, and review General Troubleshooting Information.
  • This code is caused by an electrical circuit problem and cannot be caused by a mechanical problem in the transmission.
  1. Turn the ignition switch to ON (II), and wait for 20 seconds.
  1. Check the ATF Temperature with the HDS in the A/T Data List.
Does the ATF Temperature indicate −20 °C (−4 °F) or below?
YES -
Go to Step 3 .
NO -
Go to Step 5 .
  1. Start the engine, and warm it up to normal operating temperature (the radiator fan comes on).
  1. Check the ATF Temperature with the HDS in the A/T Data List.
Does the ATF Temperature remain −20 °C (−4 °F) or below?
YES -
Replace the ATF temperature sensor, then go to Step 8 .
NO -
Intermittent failure, the system is OK at this time. If there is an abnormal temperature rise in the ATF temperature sensor, go to Step 16 .■
  1. Check the ATF Temperature with the HDS in the A/T Data List.
Does the ATF Temperature indicate 110 °C (230 °F) or higher?
YES -
Go to Step 6 .
NO -
Intermittent failure, the system is OK at this time.■
  1. Leave the engine off until the Engine Coolant Temperature reads 50 °C (122 °F) with the HDS in the A/T Data List.
  1. Check the ATF Temperature with the HDS in the A/T Data List.
Does the ATF Temperature remain 110 °C (230 °F) or higher?
YES -
Replace the ATF temperature sensor, then go to Step 8 .
NO -
Intermittent failure, the system is OK at this time. If there is an abnormal drop in the ATF temperature sensor, go to Step 16 .■
  1. Turn the ignition switch to ON (II).
  1. Clear the DTC with the HDS.
  1. Start the engine, and warm it up to normal operating temperature (the radiator fan comes on).
  1. Turn the ignition switch to LOCK (0), and allow the engine to coolant cool to the ambient air temperature (the ECT Sensor reads the same as the ambient air temperature).
  1. Block the rear wheels and raise the front of the vehicle, make sure it is securely supported, and allow the front wheels to rotate freely, or raise the vehicle on a lift.
  1. Turn the ignition switch to ON (II), and wait for 20 seconds with the ATF temperature valve between −20 °C (−4 °F) and 110 °C (230 °F). Start the engine, and warm it up to normal operating temperature (the radiator fan comes on). Start off in D, accelerate with the throttle opened at least 4 degrees, and run the vehicle at speeds over 30 km/h (19 mph) for at least 5 minutes. Slow down and stop the wheels.
  1. Check for Temporary DTCs or DTCs with the HDS.
Is DTC P0711 indicated?
YES -
Check for poor connections or loose terminals between the ATF temperature sensor and the TCM, then go to Step 1 .
NO -
Go to Step 15 .
  1. Monitor the OBD STATUS for P0711 in the DTCs MENU with the HDS.
Does the HDS indicate PASSED?
YES -
Troubleshooting is complete. If any other Temporary DTCs or DTCs were indicated in Step 14 , go to the indicated DTC's troubleshooting.■
NO -
If the HDS indicates FAILED, check for poor connections and loose terminals at the ATF temperature sensor and the TCM, then go to Step 1 . If the HDS indicates NOT COMPLETED, go to Step 10 .
  1. Update the TCM if it does not have the latest software, or substitute a known-good TCM.
  1. Turn the ignition switch to ON (II). Start the engine, and warm the engine up to normal operating temperature (the radiator fan comes on).
  1. Turn the ignition switch to LOCK (0), and allow the engine to coolant cool to the ambient air temperature (the ECT Sensor reads the same as the ambient air temperature).
  1. Block the rear wheels and raise the front of the vehicle, make sure it is securely supported, and allow the front wheels to rotate freely, or raise the vehicle on a lift.
  1. Turn the ignition switch to ON (II), and wait for 20 seconds with the ATF temperature valve between −20 °C (−4 °F) and 110 °C (230 °F). Start the engine, and warm it up to normal operating temperature (the radiator fan comes on). Start off in D, accelerate with the throttle opened at least 4 degrees, and run the vehicle at speeds over 30 km/h (19 mph) for at least 5 minutes. Slow down and stop the wheels.
  1. Check for Temporary DTCs or DTCs with the HDS.
Is DTC P0711 indicated?
YES -
Check for poor connections or loose terminals between the ATF temperature sensor and the TCM. If the TCM was updated, substitute a known-good TCM, then go to Step 17 . If the TCM was substituted, go to Step 1 .
NO -
Go to Step 22 .
  1. Monitor the OBD STATUS for P0711 in the DTCs MENU with the HDS.
Does the HDS indicate PASSED?
YES -
If the TCM was updated, troubleshooting is complete. If the TCM was substituted, replace the original TCM. If any other Temporary DTCs or DTCs were indicated in Step 21 , go to the indicated DTC's troubleshooting.■
NO -
If the HDS indicates FAILED, check for poor connections or loose terminals between the ATF temperature sensor and the TCM. If the TCM was updated, substitute a known-good TCM, then go to Step 17 . If the TCM was substituted, go to Step 1 . If the HDS indicates NOT COMPLETED, go to Step 17 .
