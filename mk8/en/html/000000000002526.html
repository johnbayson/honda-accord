DTC Troubleshooting: P0705 (5)
DTC P0705:
 Short in Transmission Range Switch Circuit (Multiple Shift-position Input)
NOTE:
  • Before you troubleshoot, record all freeze data and any on-board snapshot with the HDS, and review General Troubleshooting Information.
  • This code is caused by an electrical circuit problem and cannot be caused by a mechanical problem in the transmission.
  1. Turn the ignition switch to ON (II).
  1. Clear the DTC with the HDS.
  1. Start the engine.
  1. With the brake pedal pressed, move the shift lever through all positions. Stop for at least 1 second in each position.
  1. Monitor the OBD STATUS for P0705 in the DTCs MENU with the HDS.
Does the HDS indicate FAILED?
YES -
Go to Step 6 .
NO -
If the HDS indicates PASSED, intermittent failure, the system is OK at this time. Check for an intermittent short in the wire between the transmission range switch and the TCM. If the HDS indicates NOT COMPLETED, go to Step 3 .
  1. Turn the ignition switch to LOCK (0).
  1. Disconnect the transmission range switch connector.
  1. Turn the ignition switch to ON (II).
  1. Check the A/T P Switch, A/T R Switch, A/T N Switch, A/T D Switch, A/T S Switch, Forward Switch (ATPFWD), and Reverse Switch (ATPRVS) inputs with the HDS in the A/T data list.
Are there any ON-inputs?
YES -
Repair short in the ON-input circuit:
  • ATPP circuit: between TCM connector terminal No. 43 and transmission range switch connector terminal No. 7.
  • ATPR circuit: between TCM connector terminal No. 42 and transmission range switch connector terminal No. 8.
  • ATPN circuit: between TCM connector terminal No. 41 and transmission range switch connector terminal No. 3.
  • ATPD circuit: between TCM connector terminal No. 40 and transmission range switch connector terminal No. 9.
  • ATPS circuit: between TCM connector terminal No. 39 and transmission range switch connector terminal No. 4.
  • ATPFWD circuit: between TCM connector terminal No. 36 and transmission range switch connector terminal No. 5.
  • ATPRVS circuit: between TCM connector terminal No. 37 and transmission range switch connector terminal No. 2.
Go to Step 12 after repairing the circuit.
NO -
Go to Step 10 .
  1. Turn the ignition switch to LOCK (0).
  1. Inspect the transmission range switch.
Is the transmission range switch OK?
YES -
Connect the transmission range switch connector, then go to Step 16 .
NO -
Replace the transmission range switch, then go to Step 12 .
  1. Start the engine.
  1. With the brake pedal pressed, move the shift lever through all positions. Stop for at least 1 second in each position.
  1. Check for Temporary DTCs or DTCs with the HDS.
Is DTC P0705 indicated?
YES -
Check for intermittent short in the wire between the transmission range switch and the TCM, then go to Step 12 .
NO -
Go to Step 15 .
  1. Monitor the OBD STATUS for P0705 in the DTCs MENU with the HDS.
Does the HDS indicate PASSED?
YES -
Troubleshooting is complete. If any other Temporary DTCs or DTCs were indicated in Step 14 , go to the indicated DTC's troubleshooting.■
NO -
If the HDS indicates FAILED, check for poor connections or loose terminals between the transmission range switch and the TCM, then go to Step 1 . If the HDS indicates NOT COMPLETED, go to Step 12 .
  1. Update the TCM if it does not have the latest software, or substitute a known-good TCM.
  1. Start the engine.
  1. With the brake pedal pressed, move the shift lever through all positions. Stop for at least 1 second in each position.
  1. Check for Temporary DTCs or DTCs with the HDS.
Is DTC P0705 indicated?
YES -
Check for intermittent short in the wire between the transmission range switch and the TCM. If the TCM was updated, substitute a known-good TCM, then go to Step 17 . If the TCM was substituted, go to Step 1 .
NO -
Go to Step 20 .
  1. Monitor the OBD STATUS for P0705 in the DTCs MENU with the HDS.
Does the HDS indicate PASSED?
YES -
If the TCM was updated, troubleshooting is complete. If the TCM was substituted, replace the original TCM. If any other Temporary DTCs or DTCs were indicated in Step 19 , go to the indicated DTC's troubleshooting.■
NO -
If the HDS indicates FAILED, check for intermittent short in the wire between the transmission range switch and the TCM. If the TCM was updated, substitute a known-good TCM, then go to Step 17 . If the TCM was substituted, go to Step 1 . If the HDS indicates NOT COMPLETED, go to Step 17 .
