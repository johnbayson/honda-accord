DTC Troubleshooting: P2122
DTC P2122:
 APP Sensor A Circuit Low Voltage
NOTE: Before you troubleshoot, record all freeze data and review the general troubleshooting information.
  1. Turn the ignition switch to ON (II).
  1. Check APP SENSOR A in the DATA LIST with the HDS.
Is there about 0.2 V or less?
YES -
Go to Step 3 .
NO -
Intermittent failure, the system is OK at this time. Check for poor connections or loose terminals at APP sensor A and the ECM.■
  1. Turn the ignition switch to LOCK (0).
  1. Disconnect the APP sensor 6P connector.
  1. Turn the ignition switch to ON (II).
  1. Measure the voltage between APP sensor 6P connector terminals No. 2 and No. 3.
Is there about 5 V?
YES -
Go to Step 7 .
NO -
Go to Step 16 .
  1. Turn the ignition switch to LOCK (0), and wait 2 minutes.
  1. Disconnect ECM connector No. 2 (58P).
  1. Check for continuity between APP sensor 6P connector terminal No. 1 and body ground.
Is there continuity?
YES -
Repair short in the wire between APP sensor A and ECM connector No. 2 terminal No. 34, then go to Step 22 .
NO -
Go to Step 10 .
  1. Connect APP sensor 6P connector terminal No. 1 to body ground with a jumper wire.
  1. Check for continuity between ECM connector No. 2 terminal No. 34 and body ground.
Is there continuity?
YES -
Go to Step 12 .
NO -
Repair open in the wire between APP sensor A and ECM connector No. 2 terminal No. 34, then go to Step 22 .
  1. Reconnect ECM connector No. 2 (58P).
  1. Connect APP sensor 6P connector terminals No. 1 and No. 3 with a jumper wire.
  1. Turn the ignition switch to ON (II).
  1. Check APP SENSOR A in the DATA LIST with the HDS.
Is there about 0.2 V or less?
YES -
Go to Step 26 .
NO -
Go to Step 20 .
  1. Turn the ignition switch to LOCK (0), and wait 2 minutes.
  1. Disconnect ECM connector No. 2 (58P).
  1. Connect APP sensor 6P connector terminal No. 3 to body ground with a jumper wire.
  1. Check for continuity between ECM connector No. 2 terminal No. 37 and body ground.
Is there continuity?
YES -
Go to Step 26 .
NO -
Repair open in the wire between APP sensor A and ECM connector No. 2 terminal No. 37, then go to Step 22 .
  1. Turn the ignition switch to LOCK (0).
  1. Replace the accelerator pedal module.
  1. Reconnect all connectors.
  1. Turn the ignition switch to ON (II).
  1. Reset the ECM with the HDS.
  1. Check for Temporary DTCs or DTCs with the HDS.
Is DTC P2122 indicated?
YES -
Check for poor connections or loose terminals at APP sensor A and the ECM, then go to Step 1 .
NO -
Troubleshooting is complete. If any other Temporary DTCs or DTCs are indicated, go to the indicated DTC's troubleshooting.■
  1. Reconnect all connectors.
  1. Update the ECM if it does not have the latest software, or substitute a known-good ECM.
  1. Check for Temporary DTCs or DTCs with the HDS.
Is DTC P2122 indicated?
YES -
Check for poor connections or loose terminals at APP sensor A and the ECM. If the ECM was updated, substitute a known-good ECM, then recheck. If the ECM was substituted, go to Step 1 .
NO -
If the ECM was updated, troubleshooting is complete. If the ECM was substituted, replace the original ECM. If any other Temporary DTCs or DTCs are indicated, go to the indicated DTC's troubleshooting.■
