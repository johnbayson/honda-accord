Engine Oil Filter Replacement
Special Tools Required

Oil filter wrench, 64
 07HAA-PJ70101
  1. Remove the engine under cover.
  1. Drain the engine oil.
  1. Remove the oil filter with the oil filter wrench.
  1. Make sure the rubber seal from the old oil filter is not stuck to the oil filter seating surface of the engine.
  1. Inspect the threads (A) and the rubber seal (B) on the new oil filter. Clean the seat on the oil pan, then apply a light coat of new engine oil to the filter rubber seal. Use only filters with a built-in bypass system.
  1. Install the oil filter by hand.
  1. After the rubber seal seats, tighten the oil filter clockwise with the oil filter wrench.
    Tighten:
    3/4 Turn Clockwise
    Tightening Torque:
    12 N·m (1.2 kgf·m, 8.8 lbf·ft)

  1. If four numbers or marks (1 to 4 or ▼ to ▼▼▼▼) are printed around the outside of the filter, you can use the following procedure to tighten the filter.
    • Spin the oil filter on until its seal lightly seats against the oil pan, and note which number or mark is at the bottom.
    • Tighten the oil filter by turning it clockwise three numbers or marks from the one you noted. For example, if mark ▼ is at the bottom when the seal is lightly seated, tighten the oil filter until the mark ▼▼▼▼ comes around to the bottom.
