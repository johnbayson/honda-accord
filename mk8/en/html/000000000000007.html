Front Damper/Spring Removal and Installation
Removal
  1. Raise the front of the vehicle, and support it with safety stands in the proper locations.
  1. Remove the front wheel.
  1. Remove the wheel speed sensor harness bracket mounting bolt (A).
  1. Remove the damper pinch bolt (A) and the damper fork mounting nut (B) while holding the mounting bolt (C), then remove the damper fork (D) from the damper and the lower arm.
  1. Remove the front strut brace mounting nuts (A).
  1. Remove the damper mounting nuts (B) from the top of the damper. Do not let the damper/spring drop down under its own weight.
  1. Remove the damper/spring (A).
    NOTE: Be careful not to damage the body.
Installation
  1. Position the damper/spring (A) in the body with the aligning tab (B) facing inside.
    NOTE: Be careful not to damage the body.
  1. Loosely install the new damper mounting nuts (A) to the top of the damper.
  1. Loosely install the front strut brace mounting nuts (B).
  1. Install the damper fork (A) over the driveshaft and onto the lower arm (B). Install the aligning tab (C) on the damper unit into the slot (D) of the damper fork.
  1. Loosely install the damper pinch bolt (E) into the damper fork.
  1. Connect the damper fork and the lower arm with the new damper fork mounting bolt (F), then lightly tighten the new mounting nut (G).
  1. Place a floor jack under the lower arm, and raise the suspension to load it with the vehicle's weight.
  1. Tighten the damper pinch bolt and the damper fork mounting nut while holding the mounting bolt to the specified torque values.
  1. Tighten the damper mounting nuts and front strut brace mounting nuts on top of the damper to the specified torque values.
  1. Install the wheel speed sensor harness bracket (A).
  1. Clean the mating surfaces of the brake disc and the inside of the wheel, then install the front wheel.
  1. Check the wheel alignment, and adjust it if necessary.
