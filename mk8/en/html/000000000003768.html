DTC Troubleshooting: P0045 (172)
DTC P0045:
 Turbocharger Boost Control Solenoid Circuit Open
NOTE: Before you troubleshoot, record all freeze data and review the general troubleshooting information.
  1. Turn the ignition switch to ON (II).
  1. Clear the DTC with the HDS.
  1. Start the engine, and hold the engine speed at 2,000 rpm (min −1 ) without load (A/T in P or N, M/T in neutral).
  1. Check for Temporary DTCs or DTCs with the HDS.
Is DTC P0045 indicated?
YES -
Go to Step 5 .
NO -
Intermittent failure, the system is OK at this time. Check for poor connections or loose terminals at the turbocharger boost control solenoid valve and the ECM.■
  1. Check for Temporary DTCs or DTCs with the HDS.
Are DTC P0045, P0340, and P245A indicated at same time?
YES -
Go to Step 6 .
NO -
Go to Step 12 .
  1. Turn the ignition switch to LOCK (0).
  1. Check the No. 13 IGP2 (15 A) fuse in the under-hood fuse/relay box.
Is the fuse OK?
YES -
Go to Step 8 .
NO -
  • Check the under-hood fuse/relay box, and replace it if needed, then go to Step 21 .
  • Repair short in the wire between PGM-FI main relay 2 (SOL) and the EGR cooler bypass control solenoid valve, the turbocharger boost control solenoid valve, the CMP sensor, the reverse lock solenoid valve (M/T model). Also replace the No. 13 IGP2 (15 A) fuse, then go to Step 21 .
  1. Remove the PGM-FI main relay 2 (SOL) from the under-hood fuse/relay box.
  1. Check the PGM-FI main relay 2 (SOL).
Is PGM-FI main relay 2 (SOL) OK?
YES -
Go to Step 10 .
NO -
Replace the PGM-FI main relay 2 (SOL), then go to Step 21 .
  1. Turn the ignition switch to ON (II).
  1. Measure the voltage between PGM-FI main relay 2 (SOL) 4P connector terminal No. 2 and body ground.
Is there battery voltage?
YES -
Repair open in the wire between PGM-FI main relay 2 (SOL) and the EGR cooler bypass control solenoid valve, the turbocharger boost control solenoid valve, the CMP sensor, the reverse lock solenoid valve (M/T model), then go to Step 21 .
NO -
Check the under-hood fuse/relay box, and replace it if needed, then go to Step 21 .
  1. Turn the ignition switch to LOCK (0).
  1. Disconnect the turbocharger boost control solenoid valve 2P connector.
  1. Turn the ignition switch to ON (II).
  1. Measure the voltage between turbocharger boost control solenoid valve 2P connector terminal No. 1 and body ground.
Is there battery voltage?
YES -
Go to Step 16 .
NO -
Repair open in the wire between the PGM-FI main relay 2 (SOL) and the turbocharger boost control solenoid valve, then go to Step 21 .
  1. At solenoid side, measure the resistance between turbocharger boost control solenoid valve 2P connector terminals No. 1 and No. 2.
Is there about 10−14 Ω at 20 °C (68 °F)?
YES -
Go to Step 17
NO -
Go to Step 20 .
  1. Turn the ignition switch to LOCK (0), and wait 2 minutes.
  1. Disconnect ECM connector No. 1 (96P).
  1. Check for continuity between ECM connector No. 1 terminal No. 93 and turbocharger boost control solenoid valve 2P connector terminal No. 2.
Is there continuity?
YES -
Go to Step 26 .
NO -
Repair open in the wire between the turbocharger boost control solenoid valve and ECM connector No. 1 terminal No. 93, then go to Step 21 .
  1. Replace the turbocharger boost control solenoid valve.
  1. Reconnect all connectors.
  1. Turn the ignition switch to ON (II).
  1. Reset the ECM with the HDS.
  1. Start the engine, and hold the engine speed at 2,000 rpm (min −1 ) without load (A/T in P or N, M/T in neutral).
  1. Check for Temporary DTCs or DTCs with the HDS.
Is DTC P0045 indicated?
YES -
Check for poor connections or loose terminals at the turbocharger boost control solenoid valve and the ECM, then go to Step 1 .
NO -
Troubleshooting is complete. If any other Temporary DTCs or DTCs are indicated, go to the indicated DTC's troubleshooting.■
  1. Reconnect all connectors.
  1. Update the ECM if it does not have the latest software, or substitute a known-good ECM.
  1. Start the engine, and hold the engine speed at 2,000 rpm (min −1 ) without load (A/T in P or N, M/T in neutral).
  1. Check for Temporary DTCs or DTCs with the HDS.
Is DTC P0045 indicated?
YES -
Check for poor connections or loose terminals at the turbocharger boost control solenoid valve and the ECM. If the ECM was updated, substitute a known-good ECM, then recheck. If the ECM was substituted, go to Step 1 .
NO -
If the ECM was updated, troubleshooting is complete. If the ECM was substituted, replace the original ECM. If any other Temporary DTCs or DTCs are indicated, go to the indicated DTC's troubleshooting.■
