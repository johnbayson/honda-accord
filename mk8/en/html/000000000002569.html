DTC Troubleshooting: P1780 (49)
DTC P1780:
 Problem in Shift Control System
NOTE: Before you troubleshoot, record all freeze data and any on-board snapshot with the HDS, and review General Troubleshooting Information.
  1. Turn the ignition switch to ON (II).
  1. Clear the DTC with the HDS.
  1. Test-drive the vehicle with the shift lever in D, and let the transmission shift through all five gears. Then slow down to a stop.
  1. Check for Temporary DTCs or DTCs with the HDS.
    NOTE: DTC P1780 means there is one or more A/T DTCs stored for the shift control system.
Are there other DTCs?
YES -
Refer to the troubleshooting as indicated by the DTC.■
NO -
Go to Step 5 .
  1. Update the TCM if it does not have the latest software, or substitute a known-good TCM.
  1. Test-drive the vehicle with the shift lever in D, and let the transmission shift through all five gears. Then slow down to a stop.
  1. Check for Temporary DTCs or DTCs with the HDS.
Is DTC P1780 indicated?
YES -
If the TCM was updated, substitute a known-good TCM, then go to Step 6 . If the TCM was substituted, go to Step 1 .
NO -
Go to Step 8 .
  1. Monitor the OBD STATUS for P1780 in the DTCs MENU with the HDS.
Does the HDS indicate PASSED?
YES -
If the TCM was updated, troubleshooting is complete. If the TCM was substituted, replace the original TCM. If any other DTCs were indicated in Step 7 , go to the indicated DTC's troubleshooting.■
NO -
If the HDS indicates FAILED, check for poor connections and loose terminals at the TCM. If the TCM was updated, substitute a known-good TCM, then go to Step 6 . If the TCM was substituted, go to Step 1 . If the HDS indicates NOT COMPLETED, go to Step 6 .
