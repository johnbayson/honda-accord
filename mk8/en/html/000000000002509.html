A/T Interlock System - Shift Lock System Circuit Troubleshooting
  1. Connect the HDS to the DLC (A) located under the driver's side of the dashboard.
  1. Turn the ignition switch to ON (II). Make sure the HDS communicates with the TCM. If it does not, go to the DLC circuit troubleshooting.
  1. Select Shift Lock Solenoid Test in the Miscellaneous Test Menu, and check that the shift lock solenoid operates with the HDS.
Does the shift lock solenoid work properly?
YES -
Go to Step 15 .
NO -
Go to Step 4 .
  1. Turn the ignition switch to LOCK (0).
  1. Remove the center console.
  1. Disconnect the shift lock solenoid connector.
  1. Turn the ignition switch to ON (II).
  1. Measure the voltage between shift lock solenoid connector terminal No. 1 and body ground.
Is there battery voltage?
YES -
Go to Step 9 .
NO -
Check for a blown No. 5 (7.5 A) fuse in the driver's under-dash fuse/relay box. If the fuse is OK, repair open in the wire between shift lock solenoid connector terminal No. 1 and the driver's under-dash fuse/relay box.■
  1. Shift the shift lever in P, and press the brake pedal. Do not press the accelerator.
  1. Measure the voltage between shift lock solenoid connector terminals No. 1 and No. 2 while pressing the brake pedal.
Is there battery voltage?
YES -
Go to Step 11 .
NO -
Go to Step 12 .
  1. Release the brake pedal, and measure the voltage between shift lock solenoid connector terminals No. 1 and No. 2. The shift lever must be in P.
Is there battery voltage?
YES -
Repair short to body ground in the wire between TCM connector terminal No. 51 and the shift lock solenoid connector.■
NO -
Check the shift lock mechanism. If the mechanism is OK, replace the shift lock solenoid. ■
  1. Turn the ignition switch to LOCK (0).
  1. Disconnect the TCM connector (60P).
  1. Check for continuity between TCM connector terminal No. 51 and shift lock solenoid connector terminal No. 2.
Is there continuity?
YES -
Update the TCM if it does not have the latest software, or substitute a known-good TCM, and recheck. If the symptom goes away with a known-good TCM, replace the original TCM. ■
NO -
Repair open in the wire between TCM connector terminal No. 51 and the shift lock solenoid connector.■
  1. Press the brake pedal.
Are the brake lights ON?
YES -
Go to Step 16 .
NO -
Repair faulty brake light circuit.■
  1. Turn the ignition switch to LOCK (0).
  1. Disconnect the TCM connector (60P).
  1. Measure the voltage between TCM connector terminal No. 58 and body ground while pressing the brake pedal and when the brake pedal is released.
Is there battery voltage while pressing the brake pedal and about 0 V when the pedal is released?
YES -
Go to Step 19 .
NO -
Repair open in the wire between TCM connector terminal No. 58 and the brake pedal position switch.■
  1. Connect the TCM connector (60P).
  1. Disconnect the transmission range switch connector.
  1. Turn the ignition switch to ON (II).
  1. Measure the voltage between transmission range switch connector terminals No. 7 and No. 6.
Is there more than 5 V?
YES -
Go to Step 28 .
NO -
Go to Step 23 .
  1. Turn the ignition switch to LOCK (0).
  1. Disconnect the TCM connector (60P).
  1. Check for continuity between TCM connector terminal No. 43 and body ground.
Is there continuity?
YES -
Repair short to body ground in the wire between TCM connector terminal No. 43 and the transmission range switch connector.■
NO -
Go to Step 26 .
  1. Check for continuity between TCM connector terminal No. 43 and transmission range switch connector terminal No. 7.
Is there continuity?
YES -
Go to Step 27 .
NO -
Repair open in the wire between TCM connector terminal No. 43 and the transmission range switch connector.■
  1. Check for continuity between transmission range switch connector terminal No. 6 and body ground.
Is there continuity?
YES -
Update the TCM if it does not have the latest software, or substitute a known-good TCM, and recheck. If the symptom goes away with a known-good TCM, replace the original TCM. ■
NO -
Repair open in the wire between transmission range switch connector terminal No. 6 and body ground (G101), or repair poor body ground (G101).■
  1. Test the transmission range switch.
Is the transmission range switch OK?
YES -
Go to Step 29 .
NO -
Replace the transmission range switch. ■
