VSA System Symptom Troubleshooting - VSA activation indicator does not go off, and no DTCs are stored
VSA activation indicator does not go off, and no DTCs are stored
  1. Turn the ignition switch to ON (II).
  1. Check the VSA activation indicator for several seconds when the ignition switch is turned to ON (II).
Does the indicator come on then go off?
YES -
The system is OK at this time.■
NO -
Go to Step 3 .
  1. Turn the ignition switch to LOCK (0).
  1. Disconnect the VSA OFF switch 5P connector or VSA OFF switch 13P connector.
  1. Check the VSA OFF switch.
Is the VSA OFF switch OK?
YES -
Go to Step 6 .
NO -
Replace the VSA OFF switch. ■
  1. Disconnect the gauge control module 32P connector.
  1. Check for continuity between VSA OFF switch 5P connector terminal No. 2 or VSA OFF switch 13P connector terminal No. 11 and body ground.
Is there continuity?
YES -
Repair short to body ground between the gauge control module and the VSA OFF switch.■
NO -
Substitute a known-good gauge control module, then go to Step 1 and recheck. If it is OK, replace the original gauge control module. ■
