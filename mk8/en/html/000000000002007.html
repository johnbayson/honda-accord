Front Stabilizer Bar Replacement
NOTE: The illustrations show LHD model, RHD model is similar.
  1. Raise the front of the vehicle, and support it with safety stands in the proper locations.
  1. Remove the front wheels.
  1. Disconnect both tie-rod ball joints from the knuckle.
  1. Remove the driver's side damper fork mounting nuts (A) while holding the mounting bolt (B), then remove the mounting bolt.
    NOTE: Use the new damper fork mounting bolt and the new mounting nut, and torque the nut while holding the bolt during reassembly.
  1. Disconnect both stabilizer links from the stabilizer bar.
  1. Remove the flange bolts (A) and the bushing holders (B), then remove the bushings (C).
    NOTE:
    • During installation, align the paint marks (D) on the stabilizer bar with the side of the bushings, and note the direction for the bushings.
    • LHD model: Make sure the right and left mark (E) and direction of the bushings are correctly installed, then note direction of installation for the bushing holders.
  1. Move the stabilizer bar toward the driver's side, and remove the stabilizer bar.
  1. Install all of the removed parts in the reverse order of removal, and note these items:
    • Note the right and left direction of the stabilizer bar.
    • Refer to stabilizer link removal/installation to connect the stabilizer bar to the links.
    • Before installing the wheel, clean the mating surfaces of the brake disc and the inside of the wheel.
  1. Check the wheel alignment, and adjust it if necessary.
