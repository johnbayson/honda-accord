A/T Clutch Inspection
  1. Inspect the clutch pistons and the clutch piston check valves (A). If the clutch piston check valves are loose or damaged, replace the clutch piston.
  1. Check the spring retainer for wear and damage. If the spring retainer is worn or damaged, replace it.
  1. Inspect the clutch discs, the clutch plates, and the clutch end-plate for wear, damage, and discoloration.
    Clutch Discs
    Standard Thickness:
    1.94 mm (0.076 in.)

    Clutch Flat-plates and Clutch Wave-plates Standard Thickness
    1st Clutch:
    1.6 mm (0.063 in.)
    1st-hold Clutch:
    1.8 mm (0.071 in.)

    1.8 mm (0.071 in.)
    3rd Clutch:
    1.8 mm (0.071 in.)

    1.6 mm (0.063 in.)
    5th Clutch:
    1.6 mm (0.063 in.)

    • If the clutch discs are worn or damaged, replace them as a set. If the clutch discs are replaced, inspect the clearance between the clutch end-plate and the top disc.
    • If any clutch wave-plate is worn, damaged, or discolored, replace the damaged clutch wave-plate with a new clutch wave-plate, and inspect the other clutch wave-plates for phase difference. If the clutch wave-plate is replaced, inspect the clearance between the clutch end-plate and the top disc.
    • If any clutch flat-plate is worn, damaged, or discolored, replace as a set. If the clutch flat-plate are replaced, inspect the clearance between the clutch end-plate and the top disc.
    • If the clutch end-plate is worn, damaged, or discolored, inspect the clearance between the clutch end-plate and the top disc, then replace the clutch end-plate.
