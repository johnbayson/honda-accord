DTC Troubleshooting: B1241 (N and A/C ON)
DTC B1241 or DTC indicator N and A/C ON:
 A Problem in the Blower Motor Circuit
  1. Clear the DTC with the HDS.
  1. Turn the ignition switch to LOCK (0) and then to ON (II).
  1. Do the self-diagnostic function with the HDS or the heater control unit.
  1. Check for DTCs.
Is DTC B1241 or N and A/C ON indicated?
YES -
Go to Step 5 .
NO -
Intermittent failure; check for loose wires or poor connections on the blower motor circuit.■
  1. Turn the ignition switch to LOCK (0).
  1. Check the No. 8 (40 A) fuse in the under-hood fuse/relay box, and the No. 16 (7.5 A) fuse in the driver's under-dash fuse/relay box.
Are the fuses OK?
YES -
Go to Step 7 .
NO -
Replace the fuse(s), and recheck. If the fuse(s) blow again, check for a short in the No. 8 (40 A) fuse and the No. 16 (7.5 A) fuse circuits.■
  1. Connect the blower motor 2P connector No. 2 terminal to body ground with a jumper wire.
  1. Turn the ignition switch to ON (II).
Does the blower motor run?
YES -
Go to Step 9 .
NO -
Go to Step 25 .
  1. Turn the ignition switch to LOCK (0).
  1. Disconnect the jumper wire.
  1. Disconnect the power transistor 4P connector.
  1. Check for continuity between the power transistor 4P connector No. 2 terminal and body ground.
Is there continuity?
YES -
Go to Step 13 .
NO -
Check for an open in the BLK wire between the power transistor and body ground. If the wire is OK, check for poor ground at G302.■
  1. Connect the power transistor 4P connector No. 2 and No. 4 terminals with a jumper wire.
  1. Turn the ignition switch to ON (II).
Does the blower motor run at high speed?
YES -
Go to Step 15 .
NO -
Repair open in the GRN wire between the power transistor and the blower motor.■
  1. Turn the ignition switch to LOCK (0).
  1. Disconnect the jumper wire.
  1. Disconnect heater control unit connector B (12P).
  1. Check for continuity between body ground and heater control unit connector B (12P) No. 5 and No. 6 terminals individually.
Is there continuity?
YES -
Repair short to body ground in the wire(s) between the heater control unit and the power transistor.■
NO -
Go to Step 19 .
  1. Check for continuity between the following terminals of heater control unit connector B (12P) and the power transistor 4P connector.

    4P:

  1. Turn the ignition switch to ON (II).
  1. Measure the voltage between body ground and heater control unit connector B (12P) No. 5 and No. 6 terminals individually.
Is there voltage?
YES -
Repair short to power in the wire(s).■
NO -
Go to Step 22 .
  1. Turn the ignition switch to LOCK (0).
  1. Reconnect heater control unit connector B (12P).
  1. Test the power transistor.
Is the power transistor OK?
YES -
Check for loose wires or poor connections at heater control unit connector B (12P) and at the power transistor 4P connector. If the connections are good, substitute a known-good heater control unit, and recheck. If the symptom/indication goes away, replace the original heater control unit .■

  1. Disconnect the blower motor 2P connector.
  1. Measure the voltage between the blower motor 2P connector No. 1 terminal and body ground.
