Cam Chain Removal
NOTE: Keep the cam chain away from magnetic fields.
  1. Remove the cylinder head cover.
  1. Set up the No. 1 piston at top dead center (TDC). Align the punch mark on the intake camshaft gear in the center of the two punch marks on the exhaust camshaft gear.
  1. Remove the drive belt.
  1. Remove the idler pulley base.
  1. Remove the drive belt auto-tensioner.
  1. Remove the crankshaft pulley.
  1. Support the engine with a jack and wood block under the oil pan.
  1. Remove the torque rod stiffener (A) and upper torque rod (B).
  1. Remove the side engine mount upper bracket.
  1. Use a jack to lift the engine about 10 mm (0.4 in.).
  1. Remove the side engine mount lower bracket.
  1. Remove the chain case.
  1. Loosely install the crankshaft pulley.
  1. Hold the intake camshaft with an open-end wrench, then turn the crankshaft counterclockwise to compress the auto-tensioner.
  1. Compress the auto-tensioner fully, then insert the stopper (P/N 14511-RL0-003) or 2.5 mm (0.10 in.) diameter pin into the space between the plunger and housing. Turn the crankshaft clockwise to secure the pin.
  1. Remove the auto-tensioner.
  1. Remove the cam chain tensioner arm (A) and cam chain guide (B).
  1. Remove the cam chain.
