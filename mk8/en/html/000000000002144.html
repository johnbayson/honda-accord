DTC Troubleshooting: P108F (95)
DTC P108F:
 FRP Sensor Output High Voltage After Ignition Off
NOTE: Before you troubleshoot, record all freeze data and review the general troubleshooting information.
  1. Check for a poor connection or damage at the fuel return line.
Is the fuel return line OK?
YES -
Go to Step 2 .
NO -
Replace the fuel return line assembly, then go to Step 12 .
  1. Turn the ignition switch to ON (II).
  1. Clear the DTC with the DTC.
  1. Start the engine, then let it idle for 10 seconds.
  1. Turn the ignition switch to LOCK (0), and wait 2 minutes.
  1. Turn the ignition switch to ON (II).
  1. Check for Temporary DTCs or DTCs with the HDS.
Is DTC P108F indicated?
YES -
Go to Step 8 .
NO -
Intermittent failure, the system is OK at this time.■
  1. Disconnect the fuel return tube (A) from No. 1 cylinder, and connect a pressure side of vacuum pump/gauge (B) to the tube.
  1. Apply air to the tube.
Does it hold air?
YES -
Replace the fuel return tube assembly, then go to Step 12 .
NO -
Go to Step 10 .
  1. Turn the ignition switch to LOCK (0).
  1. Replace the fuel rail.
  1. Turn the ignition switch to ON (II).
  1. Reset the ECM with the HDS.
  1. Start the engine, then let it idle for 10 seconds.
  1. Turn the ignition switch to LOCK (0), and wait 2 minutes.
  1. Turn the ignition switch to ON (II).
  1. Check for Temporary DTCs or DTCs with the HDS.
Is DTC P108F indicated?
YES -
Check for poor connections or loose terminals at the FRP sensor and the ECM, then go to Step 1 .
NO -
Troubleshooting is complete. If any other Temporary DTCs or DTCs are indicated, go to the indicated DTC's troubleshooting.■
