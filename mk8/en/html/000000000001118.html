Crankshaft/Oil Seal (Transmission End) Installation
Special Tools Required

Driver handle, 15 x 135L
 07749-0010000

Bearing driver attachment, 24 x 26 mm
 07746-0010700

Oil seal driver attachment, 96 mm
 07ZAD-PNA0100
  1. M/T model: Install the crankshaft end bushing when replacing the crankshaft. Using the driver handle and the bearing driver attachment, drive in the crankshaft end bushing until the bearing driver attachment bottom against the crankshaft.
  1. Check the connecting rod bearing clearance with plastigage.
  1. Check the main bearing clearance with plastigage.
  1. Install the bearing halves in the engine block and the connecting rods.
  1. Apply a coat of new engine oil to the main bearings and the rod bearings.
  1. Install the CKP pulse plate (A) to the crankshaft (B).
  1. Hold the crankshaft so that rod journal No. 2 and rod journal No. 3 are straight up, and lower the crankshaft into the engine block.
  1. Apply new engine oil to the thrust washer surfaces. Install the thrust washers (A) in the No. 4 journal of the engine block.
  1. Inspect the connecting rod bolts.
  1. Apply new engine oil to the threads and flange of the connecting rod bolts.
  1. Seat the rod journals into connecting rod No. 1 and connecting rod No. 4. Line up the mark (A) on the connecting rod and the cap, then install the caps and the bolts finger-tight.
  1. Rotate the crankshaft clockwise, and seat the journals into connecting rod No. 2 and connecting rod No. 3. Line up the mark on the connecting rod and the cap, then install the caps and the bolts finger-tight.
  1. Tighten the connecting rod bolts to 20 N·m (2.0 kgf·m, 14 lbf·ft).
  1. Tighten the connecting rod bolts an additional 90 °.
    NOTE: Remove the connecting rod bolt if you tightened it beyond the specified angle, and go back to Step 9 of the procedure. Do not loosen it back to the specified angle.
  1. Remove the all of the old liquid gasket from the lower block mating surfaces, the bolts, and the bolt holes.
  1. Clean and dry the lower block mating surfaces.
  1. Apply liquid gasket (P/N 08C70-K0234M, 08C70-K0334M, or 08C70-X0331S) to the engine block mating surface of the lower block and to the inner threads of the bolt holes.
    NOTE:
    • Apply liquid gasket about 2.5 mm (0.098 in.) diameter bead along the broken line (A).
    • Do not install components if 5 minutes or more have passed after applying the liquid gasket. Instead, remove the old residue and reapply the liquid gasket.
  1. Put the lower block on the engine block.
  1. Tighten the bearing cap bolts, in sequence, to 25 N·m (2.5 kgf·m, 18 lbf·ft).
  1. Tighten the bolts, in sequence, to the specified angle.
    Specified Angle
    (1), (2), (7), (8), (9), (10):
    56 °
    (3), (4), (5), (6):
    57 °

  1. Tighten the 8 mm bolts, in sequence, to 24 N·m (2.4 kgf·m, 17 lbf·ft).
  1. Use the driver handle and the oil seal bearing driver attachment to drive a new oil seal squarely into the engine block to the specified installed height.
  1. Measure the distance between the engine block (A) and a oil seal (B).
    Oil Seal Installed Height:
    0.2−1.2 mm (0.001−0.047 in.)

  1. Install the balancer shaft/balancer shaft holder assembly.
  1. Install the oil pan.
  1. Install the cylinder head.
  1. Install the oil pump.
  1. Install the heat shield.
  1. Connect the CKP sensor connector (A), then install the CKP sensor cover (B).
  1. M/T model: Install the flywheel, clutch disc, and pressure plate.
  1. A/T model: Install the drive plate.
  1. Install the transmission:
    • Manual transmission
    • Automatic transmission
  1. Install the engine/transmission.
    NOTE: Whenever any crankshaft or connecting rod bearing is replaced, run the engine at idle speed until it reaches normal operating temperature, then continue to running it for about 15 minutes.
