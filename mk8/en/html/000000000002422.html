ATF Replacement
NOTE: Keep all foreign particles out of the transmission.
  1. Park the vehicle on level ground.
  1. Warm up the engine to normal operating temperature (the radiator fan comes on), and turn the engine off.
  1. Remove the engine undercover.
  1. Remove the drain plug (A), and drain the automatic transmission fluid (ATF).
  1. Reinstall the drain plug with a new sealing washer (B).
  1. Refill the transmission with the recommended fluid through the filler hole (A). Always use genuine Honda ATF-Z1 automatic transmission fluid (ATF). Using a non-Honda ATF can affect shift quality.
    Automatic Transmission Fluid Capacity:
    2.6 L (2.7 US qt, 2.3 Imp qt) at change
    8.2 L (8.7 US qt, 7.2 Imp qt) at overhaul

  1. Install the ATF filler bolt (B) and a new sealing washer (C).
  1. Check the ATF level.
  1. Install the engine undercover.
  1. If the Service Reminder System required to replace the ATF, go to Step 11 and reset the Service Reminder System with the HDS.
  1. Connect the HDS to the DLC (A), located under the driver's side of the dashboard.
  1. Turn the ignition switch to ON (II). Make sure the HDS communicates with the TCM. If it does not, go to the DLC circuit troubleshooting, and go to the BODY ELECTRICAL in the System Selection Menu with the HDS.
  1. Select Gauges in the BODY ELECTRICAL menu with the HDS.
  1. Select Adjustment in the Gauges menu with the HDS.
  1. Select EU Maintenance Minder in the Adjustment menu with the HDS.
  1. Select Service Reminder in the EU Maintenance Minder menu with the HDS.
  1. If the torque converter and transmission assembly are replaced as a set, go to Step 18 . If not, go to Step 19 .
  1. Select Unit Replacement in the EU Maintenance Minder menu, then select Automatic Transmission menu, and reset the ATF life with the HDS.
  1. Select Service Reminder ITEM 3, and reset the ATF life with the HDS.
