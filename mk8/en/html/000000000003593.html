A/C Magnet Valve Circuit Troubleshooting
NOTE: Do not use this troubleshooting procedure if the fans are also inoperative, or if the A/C compressor is inoperative. Refer to the symptom troubleshooting index.
  1. Check the No. 16 (7.5 A) fuse in the driver's under-dash fuse/relay box.
Is the fuse OK?
YES -
Go to Step 2 .
NO -
Replace the fuse, and recheck. If the fuse blows again, check for a short in the No. 16 (7.5 A) fuse circuit.■
  1. Disconnect the magnet valve 2P connector.
  1. Turn the ignition switch to ON (II).
  1. Measure the voltage between the magnet valve 2P connector No. 2 terminal and body ground.
Is there battery voltage?
YES -
Go to Step 5 .
NO -
Repair open in the wire between the No. 16 (7.5 A) fuse in the driver's under-dash fuse/relay box and the magnet valve.■
  1. Turn the ignition switch to LOCK (0).
  1. Reconnect the magnet valve 2P connector.
  1. Connect the magnet valve 2P connector No. 1 terminal to body ground with a jumper wire with the magnet valve connectors connected.
  1. Turn the ignition switch to ON (II).
Does the magnet valve operate?
YES -
Go to Step 9 .
NO -
Replace the magnet valve, and recheck.■
  1. Turn the ignition switch to LOCK (0).
  1. Disconnect the jumper wire.
  1. Disconnect climate control unit connector A (28P).
  1. Turn the ignition switch to ON (II).
  1. Measure the voltage between climate control unit connector B (12P) No. 17 terminal and body ground.
Is there battery voltage?
YES -
Check for loose wires or poor connections at climate control unit connector B (12P) and the magnet valve 2P connector. If the connections are good, substitute a known-good climate control unit, and recheck. If the symptom/indication goes away, replace the original climate control unit .■
NO -
Repair open in the wire between the climate control unit and the magnet valve.■
