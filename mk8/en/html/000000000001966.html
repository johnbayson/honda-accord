Fuel Rail Removal and Installation
Be careful not to jet when you removed or disconnected the high pressure line. Place a rag or shop towel over the high pressure line whenever you disconnect the high pressure line.

Removal
  1. Remove the engine cover.
  1. Remove the strut brace.
  1. Remove the bolt (A). Disconnect the glow plug connectors (B), the fuel injector connectors (C), the turbocharger boost control solenoid valve connector (D), the FRP control valve connector (E), the FPR sensor connector (F), and the FRP regulator connector (G), then raise the harness holder (H).
  1. Remove the bracket (A).
  1. Remove the high pressure pipes (B), and disconnect the fuel rail return hose (C).
    NOTE: When removing the No. 2 high pressure pipe, remove the high pressure pipe cover (D).
  1. Remove the fuel rail (E).
Installation
  1. Remove the injectors.
  1. Install the fuel rail (A) and connect the fuel rail return hose (B), then install the bracket (C).
  1. Position the injector angle, then tighten the nuts (D) of the fuel rail side and the nuts (E) of the injector side by the hand with a new high pressure pipes.
  1. Install the nozzle arm (F), and the injector with a new gasket (G), then tighten the nozzle arm nut (H) to 5 N·m (0.5 kgf·m, 4 lbf·ft).
    NOTE: Coat the nut with clean engine oil.
  1. Tighten the nuts of the injector side to 33 N·m (3.4 kgf·m, 25 lbf·ft).
  1. Tighten the nuts of the fuel rail side to 33 N·m (3.4 kgf·m, 25 lbf·ft).
  1. Tighten the nozzle arm nut to 5 N·m (0.5 kgf·m, 4 lbf·ft). Using a beam-type torque wrench. When using a preset-type torque wrench, be sure to tighten slowly and do not overtighten. If a bolt makes any noise while you are torquing it, loosen the bolt and retighten it from the first step.
  1. After torquing, tighten the nozzle arm nut to 90 °, then hold the injector. If you are using a new nozzle arm nut, tighten the nut to 100 °.
    NOTE:
    • When removing the nozzle arm nut, check the torque of injector bolt (I).
    • The high pressure pipe can not be reused once it has been removed or loosened. Replace the high pressure pipe.
  1. Loosen the high pressure fuel pump.
  1. Position the high pressure fuel pump, then tighten the nuts (A) of the fuel rail side and the nuts (B) of the high pressure fuel pump side by the hand with a new high pressure pipes (C).
  1. Tighten the high pressure fuel pump (D).
  1. Tighten the nuts of high pressure fuel pump side to 33 N·m (3.4 kgf·m, 25 lbf·ft).
  1. Tighten the nuts of fuel rail side to 33 N·m (3.4 kgf·m, 25 lbf·ft).
  1. Install the high pressure pipe clamps and rubbers (E).
    NOTE: If the high pressure pipe clamp and rubber are not installed, fuel may leak from fuel pipe.
  1. Connect the injector fuel return hose, the glow plug connectors, the fuel injector connectors, the turbocharger boost control solenoid valve connector, the FRP control valve connector, the FPR sensor connector, and the FRP regulator connector, and the harness holder.
  1. Do the DPF regeneration.
  1. Start the engine, then check that there is no leakage in the fuel supply system.
  1. Install the bolt, the high pressure pipe cover, and the engine cover.
  1. Install the strut brace.
