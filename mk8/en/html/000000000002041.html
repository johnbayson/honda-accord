LKAS Symptom Troubleshooting - LKAS indicator (amber) does not go off
LKAS indicator (amber) does not go off
  1. Turn the ignition switch to LOCK (0).
  1. Connect the HDS to the data link connector (DLC).
  1. Check for DTCs with the HDS.
Is any DTC indicated?
YES -
Troubleshoot the indicated DTC.■
NO -
Go to Step 4 .
  1. Clear the DTCs with the HDS.
  1. Start the engine, and wait for 2 seconds.
  1. Watch the LKAS indicator.
Does the LKAS indicator go off?
YES -
Intermittent failure. The system is OK at this time. Check for loose or poor connections.■
NO -
Go to Step 7 .
  1. Turn the ignition switch to LOCK (0), then turn it to ON (II).
  1. Check for ACC system DTCs with the HDS.
Is any DTC indicated?
YES -
Troubleshoot the indicated DTC.■
NO -
Go to Step 9 .
  1. Check for ECM DTCs with the HDS.
Is DTC U0104 indicated?
YES -
Go to Step 10 .
NO -
Go to the gauge control module self-diagnostic function. ■
  1. Check for VSA system DTCs with the HDS.
Is DTC 86 indicated?
YES -
Go to Step 11 .
NO -
Go to the gauge control module self-diagnostic function. ■
  1. Check for ACC unit DTCs with the HDS.
Is DTC 107 indicated?
YES -
Aim the millimeter wave radar. ■
NO -
Go to the ACC system troubleshooting. If OK, update the ACC system, then recheck. If the ACC unit was already updated, replace the ACC unit. ■
