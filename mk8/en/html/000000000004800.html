Engine Removal
Special Tools Required

Engine tilt hanger set
 07KAK-SJ40101

Engine hanger stay
 07KAK-SJ40200

Sub hanger stay
 07MAK-PY30100
These tools use only for except EU models.
NOTE:
  • Use fender covers to avoid damaging painted surfaces.
  • To avoid damaging the wiring and terminals, unplug the wiring connectors carefully while holding the connector portion.
  • Mark all wiring and hoses to avoid misconnection. Also, be sure that they do not contact other wiring or hoses, or interfere with other parts.
  1. Secure the hood in the wide open position (support rod in the lower hole).
  1. Do the battery removal procedure .
  1. Remove the air cleaner housing assembly.
  1. Remove the strut brace.
  1. Remove the engine cover.
  1. Remove the connector cover (A) and the harness clamps (B), then disconnect the engine control module (ECM) connectors (C).
  1. Remove the intake air duct.
  1. Remove the ground cable (A) and the harness clamp (B), then remove the ECM/bracket assembly (C).
  1. Remove the harness holder (A) and the harness clamps (B), then disconnect the engine wire harness connector (C).
  1. Remove the battery cable (A) from the under-hood fuse/relay box (B), then remove the under-hood fuse/relay box.
  1. Remove the harness clamps (C) and the air cleaner housing bracket (D).
  1. Remove the harness clamp (A). Remove the two bolts (B) and loosen the two bolts (C), then remove the battery base (D).
  1. Remove the shift cables.
  1. Remove the clutch slave cylinder and the clutch line bracket mounting nut.
  1. Remove the fuel hose (A), then disconnect the fuel return hose (B) and the brake booster vacuum hose (C).
  1. Remove the drive belt.
  1. Remove the radiator cap.
  1. Raise the vehicle on the lift.
  1. Remove the front wheels.
  1. Remove the splash shield.
  1. Loosen the drain plug in the radiator, and drain the engine coolant.
  1. Drain the engine oil.
  1. Drain the transmission fluid.
  1. Remove exhaust pipe A.
  1. Remove the damper fork.
  1. Separate the knuckles from the lower arms.
  1. Remove the driveshafts. Coat all precision-finished surfaces with new engine oil. Tie a plastic bags over the driveshaft ends.
  1. Remove the lower torque rod.
  1. Lower the vehicle on the lift.
  1. Disconnect the intercooler outlet hose (A), the turbocharger outlet hose (B), and the water bypass hose (C).
  1. Disconnect the heater hoses.
  1. Remove the radiator.
  1. Remove the two bolts securing the expansion tank.
  1. Disconnect the A/C compressor clutch connector (A), then remove the A/C compressor (B) without disconnecting the A/C hoses.
  1. Support the engine with a jack and a wood block under the oil pan.
  1. Remove the torque rod stiffener (A), the upper torque rod (B) and the ground cable (C).
  1. Remove the side engine mount upper bracket.
  1. Install the engine hanger to the side engine mount lower bracket. Attach the special tools (Commercially available tool for EU models) to the engine/transmission as shown.
  1. Remove the ground cable (A), then remove the transmission mount (B).
    NOTE: Do not remove the TORX bolt (C) from the upper transmission mount. If the TORX bolt is removed, the upper transmission mount must be replaced as an assembly.
  1. Check that the engine/transmission is completely free of vacuum hoses, fuel and coolant hoses, and electrical wiring.
  1. Slowly raise the engine/transmission assembly about 150 mm (6 in.). Check once again that all hoses and electrical wiring are disconnected and free from the engine/transmission.
  1. Raise the engine all the way, and remove it from the vehicle.
