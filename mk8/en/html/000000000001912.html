Engine Oil Pan Removal
NOTE: Make sure to inspect the oil pump chain, when removing the oil pan.
  1. If the engine is out of the vehicle, go to Step 19 .
  1. Remove the strut brace.
  1. Remove the drive belt.
  1. Disconnect the diesel particulate filter (DPF) differential pressure sensor connector, and remove the DPF differential pressure sensor from the bracket.
  1. Remove the bolts securing the water bypass line. Disconnect the A/C condenser fan motor connector, then remove the A/C condenser fan/shroud assembly.
  1. Disconnect the A/C compressor clutch connector, then remove the A/C compressor without disconnecting the A/C hoses.
  1. Remove the steering gearbox mounting bolts.
  1. Remove the splash shield.
  1. Drain the engine oil.
  1. With HID model: Disconnect the headlight front leveling sensor connector.
  1. Remove exhaust pipe A.
  1. Remove the damper fork mounting bolts.
  1. Support the transmission with a jack.
  1. Remove the lower torque rod.
  1. Remove the jack.
  1. Remove the subframe middle mounts.
  1. Support the rear side of the subframe with a jack. Remove the subframe stiffener mounting bolts (A) on both sides, then loosen the subframe mounting bolts (B) on the rear side of subframe so they are about 15 mm (0.6 in.) from the mounting surface. Remove the jack.
  1. Support the front side of the subframe with a jack. Remove the subframe stiffener mounting bolts (A) and subframe mounting bolts (B). Lower the subframe so it is about 50 mm (2.0 in.) from the body.
  1. Remove the DPF brackets.
  1. Remove the A/C compressor bracket.
  1. Remove the lower torque rod bracket.
  1. Remove the harness holder bracket.
  1. Remove the oil level sensor cover (A), then disconnect the oil level sensor connector (B).
  1. Remove the clutch cover (A) and transmission mounting bolts (B).
  1. Remove the bolts securing the oil pan.
  1. Using a flat blade screwdriver, separate the oil pan from the lower block in the places shown.
  1. Remove the oil pan.
  1. Inspect the oil pump chain.
