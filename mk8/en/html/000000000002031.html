A/C Condenser Fan High Speed Circuit Troubleshooting
NOTE:
  • Do not use this troubleshooting procedure if the A/C compressor is inoperative. Refer to the symptom troubleshooting index.
  • Before performing symptom troubleshooting, check for powertrain DTCs.
  1. Remove the relay circuit board from the under-hood fuse/relay box, and test the A/C condenser fan relay.
Is the relay OK?
YES -
Go to Step 2 .
NO -
Replace the relay circuit board.■
  1. Jump the SCS line with the HDS.
    NOTE: This step must be done to protect the engine control module (ECM) from damage.
  1. Disconnect ECM connector No. 2 (58P).
  1. Turn the ignition switch to ON (II).
  1. Measure the voltage between the ECM connector No. 2 (58P) No. 18 terminal and body ground.
Is there battery voltage?
YES -
Go to Step 6 .
NO -
Repair open in the wire between the under-hood fuse/relay box and the ECM. If the wire is OK, replace the under-hood fuse/relay box. ■
  1. Turn the ignition switch to LOCK (0).
  1. Connect the ECM connector No. 2 (58P) No. 18 terminal to body ground with a jumper wire.
  1. Turn the ignition switch to ON (II).
Does the A/C condenser fan run on high?
YES -
Check for loose wire or poor connection at ECM connector No. 2 (58P) No. 18 terminal. If the connection is good, substitute a known-good ECM, and recheck. If the symptom/indication goes away, replace the original ECM. ■
NO -
Check for an open in the wire between under-hood fuse/relay box connector B (14P) No. 7 terminal and body ground. If the wire is OK, check for poor ground at G301. ■
