Carpet Replacement
SRS components are located in this area. Review the SRS component locations and the precautions and procedures before doing repairs or service.
NOTE:
  • Put on gloves to protect your hands.
  • Take care not to damage, wrinkle or twist the carpet.
  • Be careful not to damage the dashboard and other interior trim pieces.
  • When removing components, use the trim tools or equivalent.
  1. Remove the front seat mounting bolts.
  1. Do the battery terminal disconnection procedure, then wait at least 3 minutes before beginning work.
  1. Remove these items:
    • Front seats, both sides
    • Rear seat cushion
    • Front door sill trim, both sides
    • Rear door sill trim, both sides
    • Front door opening seal, as needed
    • Rear door opening seal, as needed
    • Kick panels, both sides
    • Center pillar lower trim
    • Driver's dashboard undercover
    • Passenger's dashboard undercover, for some models
    • Center console panel
    • Center console rear trim
    • Driver's center trim
    • Passenger's dashboard trim
    • Passenger's center trim
    • Center console
    • Rear ventilation ducts, both sides, for some models
    • Steering joint cover
    • Parking brake lever
    • Driver's dashboard center lower cover
    • Passenger's dashboard center lower cover
    • M/T model: Shift lever bracket
    • A/T model: Shift lever
  1. Remove the front passenger's seat belt lower anchor bolt.
  1. Release the hooks (A), then remove the accelerator pedal clip (B).
  1. While pushing down the back-up spring (A), pull back the accelerator pedal (B) to release the hook (C).
  1. Remove the footrest (A) by pulling it to detach the clips.

RHD model
  1. Disconnect the floor wire harness connector (A) on some RHD models, the yaw rate-lateral acceleration sensor connector (B), and if equipped, the USB adapter unit connector (C), and detach the wire harness clips (D). Using a TORX T30 bit, remove the ground bolt (E).

  1. A/T model: Remove the bolt, then remove the shift lever mount bracket (A).
  1. Disconnect the floor wire harness connector (A), and detach the harness clips (B) fastening the floor wire harness (C).
LHD model
RHD model
  1. Release the Velcro fastener (A) (for some models), and release the carpet (B) from the hooks (C) of both rear heater ducts. Remove the clips (D). Detach the clips (E) fastening the carpet to both door sill areas:
    • Replace the clips (E) with new ones.
    • LHD model is shown; RHD model is symmetrical.
  1. Pull the carpet out from under the dashboard, then remove the carpet. Pull the seat harnesses (F) out through the holes in the carpet. LHD model is shown; RHD model is similar.
  1. Install the carpet in the reverse order of removal, and note these items:
    • Take care not to damage, wrinkle or twist the carpet.
    • Make sure the seat harnesses are routed correctly.
    • Slip the holes in the carpet over the rear heater ducts.
    • If the clips fastening the carpet to the floor are damaged or stress-whitened, replace them with new ones.
    • Replace the clips fastening the carpet to both door sill areas with new ones.
    • Push the Velcro fastener and the clips into place securely.
    • Do the battery terminal reconnection procedure.
    • If necessary, adjust the parking brake cable.
    • Push the accelerator pedal hook into place securely, and after installing, make sure the accelerator pedal does not come the floor by pulling it back.
