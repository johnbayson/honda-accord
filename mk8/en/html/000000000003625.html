DTC Troubleshooting: P0651 (134)
DTC P0651:
 Sensor Reference Voltage B Circuit Malfunction
NOTE: Before you troubleshoot, record all freeze data and review the general troubleshooting information.
  1. Turn the ignition switch to ON (II).
  1. Clear the DTC with the HDS.
  1. Check for Temporary DTCs or DTCs with the HDS.
Is DTC P0651 indicated?
YES -
Go to Step 4 .
NO -
Intermittent failure, the system is OK at this time. Check for poor connections or loose terminals at the IMRC valve, the output shaft (countershaft) speed sensor, the turbocharger boost control valve position sensor, the DPF differential pressure sensor, and the ECM.■
  1. Turn the ignition switch to LOCK (0), and wait 2 minutes.
  1. Disconnect the connectors from these sensors:
    • IMRC valve
    • Output shaft (countershaft) speed sensor
    • Turbocharger boost control valve position sensor
    • DPF differential pressure sensor
  1. Turn the ignition switch to ON (II).
  1. Clear the DTC with the HDS.
  1. While connecting each of the parts below, one at a time, check for Temporary DTCs or DTCs with the HDS.
    • IMRC valve
    • Output shaft (countershaft) speed sensor
    • Turbocharger boost control valve position sensor
    • DPF differential pressure sensor
Was DTC P0651 indicated when one of the above parts is connected?
YES -
Replace the sensor that indicated DTC when connected, then go to Step 22 .
NO -
Go to Step 9 .
  1. Turn the ignition switch to LOCK (0), and wait 2 minutes.
  1. Disconnect ECM connectors No. 1 (96P) and No. 2 (58P).
  1. Turn the ignition switch to ON (II).
  1. Measure the voltage between body ground and ECM connector No. 1 terminals No. 18, No. 42, No. 44, and ECM connector No. 2 terminal No. 11 individually.
Is there battery voltage?
YES -
Repair short to power in the wire between the ECM connector terminals and these sensors, then go to Step 22 .
  • IMRC valve and ECM connector No. 1 terminal No. 44
  • Output shaft (countershaft) speed sensor and ECM connector No. 1 terminal No. 18
  • Turbocharger boost control valve position sensor and ECM connector No. 1 terminal No. 42
  • DPF differential pressure sensor and ECM connector No. 2 terminal No. 11
NO -
Go to Step 13 .
  1. Turn the ignition switch to LOCK (0).
  1. Disconnect the connectors from these sensors:
    • IMRC valve
    • Output shaft (countershaft) speed sensor
    • Turbocharger boost control valve position sensor
    • DPF differential pressure sensor
  1. Check for continuity between ECM connector No. 2 terminal No. 1 and IMRC valve 5P connector terminal No. 1.
Is there continuity?
YES -
Repair short in the wire between the IMRC position and ECM connector No. 2 terminal No. 1, No. 3, or No. 5, then go to Step 23 .
NO -
Go to Step 16 .
  1. Connect output shaft (countershaft) speed sensor 3P connector terminal No. 1 to body ground with a jumper wire.
  1. Check for continuity between ECM connector No. 2 terminal No. 1 and body ground.
Is there continuity?
YES -
Repair short in the wire between the output shaft (countershaft) speed sensor and ECM connector No. 2 terminal No. 1, No. 3, or No. 5 then go to Step 23 .
NO -
Go to Step 18 .
  1. Remove the jumper wire from the output shaft (countershaft) speed sensor 3P connector.
  1. Check for continuity between ECM connector No. 2 terminal No. 1 and turbocharger boost control valve position sensor 3P connector terminal No. 1.
Is there continuity?
YES -
Repair short in the wire between the turbocharger boost control valve position sensor and ECM connector No. 2 terminal No. 1, No. 3, or No. 5, then go to Step 23 .
NO -
Go to Step 20 .
  1. Check for continuity between ECM connector No. 2 terminal No. 1 and DPF differential pressure sensor 3P connector terminal No. 3.
Is there continuity?
YES -
Repair short in the wire between the DPF differential pressure sensor and ECM connector No. 2 terminal No. 1, No. 3, or No. 5, then go to Step 23 .
NO -
Go to Step 21 .
  1. Check for continuity between body ground and ECM connector No. 1 terminals No. 18, No. 42, No. 44, and ECM connector No. 2 terminal No. 11 individually.
Is there continuity?
YES -
Repair short in the wire between the ECM connector terminals and these sensors, then go to Step 23 .
  • IMRC valve and ECM connector No. 1 terminal No. 44
  • Output shaft (countershaft) speed sensor and ECM connector No. 1 terminal No. 18
  • Turbocharger boost control valve position sensor and ECM connector No. 1 terminal No. 42
  • DPF differential pressure sensor and ECM connector No. 2 terminal No. 11
NO -
Go to Step 27 .
  1. Turn the ignition switch to LOCK (0).
  1. Reconnect all connectors.
  1. Turn the ignition switch to ON (II).
  1. Reset the ECM with the HDS.
  1. Check for Temporary DTCs or DTCs with the HDS.
Is DTC P0651 indicated?
YES -
Check for poor connections or loose terminals at the IMRC valve, the output shaft (countershaft) speed sensor, the turbocharger boost control valve position sensor, the DPF differential pressure sensor, and the ECM, then go to Step 1 .
NO -
Troubleshooting is complete. If any other Temporary DTCs or DTCs are indicated, go to the indicated DTC's troubleshooting.■
  1. Reconnect all connectors.
  1. Update the ECM if it does not have the latest software, or substitute a known-good ECM.
  1. Check for Temporary DTCs or DTCs with the HDS.
Is DTC P0651 indicated?
YES -
Check for poor connections or loose terminals at the IMRC valve, the output shaft (countershaft) speed sensor, the turbocharger boost control valve position sensor, the DPF differential pressure sensor, and the ECM. If the ECM was updated, substitute a known-good ECM, then recheck. If the ECM was substituted, go to Step 1 .
NO -
If the ECM was updated, troubleshooting is complete. If the ECM was substituted, replace the original ECM. If any other Temporary DTCs or DTCs are indicated, go to the indicated DTC's troubleshooting.■
