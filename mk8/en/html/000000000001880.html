Radiator Fan High Speed Circuit Troubleshooting
  1. Check the No. 3−6 (30 A) fuse in the under-hood fuse/relay box.
Is the fuses OK?
YES -
Go to Step 2 .
NO -
Replace the fuse and recheck.■
  1. Remove the relay circuit board from the under-hood fuse/relay box, and test the radiator fan relay.
Is the relay OK?
YES -
Go to Step 3 .
NO -
Replace the relay circuit board and recheck.■
  1. Test the radiator fan motor.
Is the motor OK?
YES -
Go to Step 4 .
NO -
Replace the radiator fan motor. ■
  1. Disconnect the radiator fan motor 2P connector and the under-hood fuse/relay box connector D.
  1. Check for continuity between under-hood fuse/relay box connector D7 and the radiator fan motor 2P connector terminal No. 2.
Is there continuity?
YES -
Go to Step 6 .
NO -
Repair open in the wire between the under-hood fuse/relay box and the radiator fan motor 2P connector terminal No. 2.■
  1. Check for continuity between the radiator fan motor 2P connector terminal No. 1 and body ground.
Is there continuity?
YES -
Replace the under-hood fuse/relay box.■
NO -
Check for open in the wire between the radiator fan motor 2P connector terminal No. 1 and body ground. If the wire is OK, check for poor ground at G301.■
