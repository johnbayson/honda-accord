M/T Assembly Removal
Special Tools Required

Subhanger stay
 07MAK-PY30100

Engine support hanger
 AAR-T1256-J00
*
*: This special tool is available from Snap-on Tools    International, LLC.
NOTE: Use fender covers to avoid damaging painted surfaces.
  1. Secure the hood in the wide open position with the support strut.
  1. Do the battery removal procedure.
  1. Remove the intake air duct.
  1. Remove the air cleaner housing.
  1. Remove the front grille cover.
  1. Remove the strut brace (if equipped).
  1. Disconnect the ECM connectors, then remove the ECM.
  1. Remove the splash shield.
  1. Remove the condenser fan shroud assembly and the radiator fan shroud assembly.
  1. Remove the clutch hose bracket (A) and the slave cylinder (B), then carefully move the slave cylinder out of the way to avoid bending the clutch line.
    NOTE:
    • Do not disconnect the clutch line joints.
    • Do not press the clutch pedal after the slave cylinder has been removed.
  1. Remove the nut (A), and release the clamp (B), then move the under-hood fuse/relay box (C) out of the way.
  1. Remove the lock pins (A) and the shift cable bracket bolts (B), then disconnect the shift cables (C) from the select lever (D) and the change lever (E). Carefully remove both cables and the shift cable bracket (F) together to avoid bending the cables.
  1. Disconnect the output shaft (countershaft) speed sensor connector (A).
  1. Disconnect the back-up light switch connector (B), and remove transmission hanger D from the transmission housing.
  1. Remove the rear side steering gearbox mounting bolts and the stiffener (A).
    NOTE: The washers (B) are on the bottom of the steering gearbox mounts.
  1. Remove the front side steering gearbox mounting bolts (A) and the washers (B).
  1. Hang up both sides of the steering gearbox end securely to prevent damage the EPS harnesses.
  1. Remove the engine cover.

Passenger's side
  1. Attach the subhanger stay (A) to threaded hole in the vacuum pump bracket using a 10 x 1.25 mm bolt (B).
  1. Install the engine support hanger (AAR-T1256-J00), then attach the hook to the slotted hole in the subhanger stay. Tighten the wing nut (A) by hand to lift and support the engine/transmission.
  1. Remove the engine harness clamps (A) from the side of the engine room.
  1. Remove the ground cable (A) from the transmission mount.
  1. Remove the transmission mount bolt and nuts from the transmission housing.
    NOTE: Do not remove the TORX bolt (B) from the upper transmission mount. If the TORX bolt is removed, the upper transmission mount must be replace as an assembly.
  1. Remove the transmission mount bolts from the body frame, then remove the transmission mount (A).
    NOTE: Slightly lower the engine/transmission supported by the engine support hanger to remove the transmission mount if it necessary.
  1. Disconnect the reverse lockout solenoid connector (A).
  1. Remove the engine harness clamps (A) from the harness bracket (B).
  1. Remove the upper transmission mounting bolts (C) and the harness bracket.
  1. Raise the vehicle on a lift, and make sure it is securely supported.
  1. Remove the front wheels.
  1. With HID lamp model: Disconnect the front suspension stroke sensor connector.
  1. Remove the damper fork.
  1. Separate the knuckle ball joint from the lower arm.
  1. Drain the MTF. Reinstall the drain plug with a new sealing washer.
  1. Remove exhaust pipe A and the gaskets.
  1. Remove the lower torque rod connecting bolts (A) from the bracket on the engine block.
  1. Remove both sides of the front subframe mid mount bolts (A).
  1. Disconnect the EPS ground subharness connector at the front of the front subframe.
  1. Support the front subframe with a piece of wood and a jack.
  1. Remove the six stiffeners mounting bolts (A), then remove the four front subframe mounting bolts (B) and the stiffeners (C).
  1. Remove the front subframe (D).
    NOTE: Pick up the steering gearbox mounting washers (E).
  1. Pry the left driveshaft inboard joint (A) from the differential using a pryber.
    NOTE:
    • Do not pull on the driveshaft, or the inboard joint may come apart. Pull the inboard joint straight out to avoid damaging the oil seal.
    • Be careful not to damage the oil seal with the prybar.
  1. Drive the inboard joint (A) of the right driveshaft off of the intermediate shaft using a drift and a hammer.
    NOTE: Do not pull on the driveshaft, or the inboard joint may come apart.
  1. Remove the intermediate shaft.
  1. Remove the clutch case cover (A).
  1. Securely support the transmission with a transmission jack.
  1. Remove the DPF converter mounting bracket (A).
  1. Remove the lower transmission mounting bolts.
  1. Pull the transmission away from the engine until the transmission mainshaft clears the clutch pressure plate.
  1. Slowly lower the transmission about 150 mm (6 in.). Check once again that all hoses and harnesses are disconnected and free from the transmission, then lower it completely.
