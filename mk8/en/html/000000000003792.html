DTC Troubleshooting: P2237 (48)
DTC P2237:
 A/F Sensor 1 Positive Current Control Circuit Open
NOTE: Before you troubleshoot, record all freeze data and review the general troubleshooting information.
  1. Turn the ignition switch to ON (II).
  1. Clear the DTC with the HDS.
  1. Start the engine, and hold the engine speed at 2,000 rpm (min −1 ) without load (A/T in P or N, M/T in neutral) until the radiator fan comes on, then let it idle.
  1. Test-drive under these conditions:
    • Engine coolant temperature (ECT SENSOR 1) above 70 °C (158 °F)
    • A/T in D, M/T in 3rd or 4th
    • Drive at a steady speed 60 km/h (38 mph) or more, then decelerate (with throttle fully closed) for 7 seconds
  1. Monitor the OBD STATUS for DTC P2237 in the DTCs MENU with the HDS.
Does the screen indicate FAILED?
YES -
Go to Step 6 .
NO -
If the screen indicates PASSED, intermittent failure, the system is OK at this time. Check for poor connections or loose terminals at A/F sensor 1 and the ECM. If the screen indicates EXECUTING, keep driving until a result comes on. If the screen indicates OUT OF CONDITION, go to Step 3 and recheck.
  1. Turn the ignition switch to LOCK (0).
  1. Disconnect the A/F sensor 1 6P connector.
  1. Connect A/F sensor 1 6P connector terminal No. 6 to body ground with a jumper wire.
  1. Turn the ignition switch to ON (II).
  1. Clear the DTC with the HDS.
  1. Start the engine, and hold the engine speed at 2,500 rpm (min −1 ) without load (A/T in P or N, M/T in neutral) for 3 minutes, then let it idle.
  1. Check for Temporary DTCs or DTCs with the HDS.
Is DTC P0131 indicated?
YES -
Go to Step 17 .
NO -
Go to Step 13 .
  1. Turn the ignition switch to LOCK (0), and wait 2 minutes.
  1. Remove the jumper wire from the A/F sensor 1 6P connector.
  1. Disconnect ECM connector No. 1 (96P).
  1. Check for continuity between ECM connector No. 1 terminal No. 87 and A/F sensor 1 6P connector terminal No. 6.
Is there continuity?
YES -
Go to Step 26 .
NO -
Repair open in the wire between A/F sensor 1 and ECM connector No. 1 terminal No. 87, then go to Step 19 .
  1. Turn the ignition switch to LOCK (0).
  1. Replace A/F sensor 1.
  1. Reconnect all connectors.
  1. Turn the ignition switch to ON (II).
  1. Reset the ECM with the HDS.
  1. Start the engine, and hold the engine speed at 2,000 rpm (min −1 ) without load (A/T in P or N, M/T in neutral) until the radiator fan comes on, then let it idle.
  1. Test-drive under these conditions:
    • Engine coolant temperature (ECT SENSOR 1) above 70 °C (158 °F)
    • A/T in D, M/T in 3rd or 4th
    • Drive at a steady speed 60 km/h (38 mph) or more, then decelerate (with throttle fully closed) for 7 seconds
  1. Check for Temporary DTCs or DTCs with the HDS.
Is DTC P2237 indicated?
YES -
Check for poor connections or loose terminals at A/F sensor 1 and the ECM, then go to Step 1 .
NO -
Go to Step 25 .

NO -

YES -

YES -
If the ECM was updated, troubleshooting is complete. If the ECM was substituted, replace the original ECM. If any other Temporary DTCs or DTCs were indicated in Step 30 , go to the indicated DTC's troubleshooting.■
NO -
