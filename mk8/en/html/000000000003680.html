Radiator and A/C Condenser Fan Low Speed Circuit Troubleshooting
NOTE:
  • Do not use this troubleshooting procedure if the A/C compressor is inoperative. Refer to the symptom troubleshooting index.
  • Before performing symptom troubleshooting, check for powertrain DTCs.
  1. Check the No. 2-6 (SUB FAN MTR) (30 A) fuse (A/T) or the No. 17 (30 A) fuse (M/T) in the under-hood fuse/relay box.
Are you fuses OK?
YES -
Go to Step 2 .
NO -
Replace the fuse, and recheck. If the fuse blow again, check for a short in the No. 2-6 (SUB FAN MTR) (30 A) fuse (A/T) or the No. 17 (30 A) fuse (M/T) circuits.■
  1. Remove the A/C condenser fan relay from the under-hood fuse/relay box, and test it.
Is the relay OK?
YES -
Go to Step 3 .
NO -
Replace the A/C condenser fan relay .■
  1. Measure the voltage between the A/C condenser fan relay 4P socket No. 1 terminal and body ground.
Is there battery voltage?
YES -
Go to Step 4 .
NO -
Replace the under-hood fuse/relay box. ■
  1. Connect the A/C condenser fan relay 4P socket No. 1 and No. 2 terminals with a jumper wire.
Does the A/C condenser and radiator fans run on low?
YES -
Go to Step 5 .
NO -
Go to Step 14 .
  1. Disconnect the jumper wire.
  1. Turn the ignition switch to ON (II).
  1. Measure the voltage between the A/C condenser fan relay 4P socket No. 4 terminal and body ground.
Is there battery voltage?
YES -
Go to Step 8 .
NO -
Replace the under-hood fuse/relay box. ■
  1. Turn the ignition switch to LOCK (0).
  1. Reinstall the A/C condenser fan relay.
  1. Jump the SCS line with the HDS.
    NOTE: This step must be done to protect the engine control module (ECM) from damage.
  1. Disconnect ECM connector No. 2 (58P).
  1. Connect ECM connector No. 2 (58P) No. 19 terminal to body ground with a jumper wire.
  1. Turn the ignition switch to ON (II).
Do the A/C condenser and radiator fans run on low?
YES -
Check for loose wire or poor connection at ECM connector No. 2 (58P) No. 19 terminal. If the connection is good, substitute a known-good ECM, and recheck. If the symptom/indication goes away, replace the original ECM. ■
NO -
Repair open in the wire between the A/C condenser fan relay and the ECM.■
  1. Disconnect the jumper wire.
  1. Reinstall the A/C condenser fan relay.
  1. Disconnect the A/C condenser fan 2P connector.
  1. Turn the ignition switch to ON (II), then set the A/C button and fan control button ON.
  1. Measure the voltage between the A/C condenser fan 2P connector No. 2 terminal and body ground.
Is there battery voltage?
YES -
Go to Step 19 .
NO -
Repair open in the wire between the A/C condenser fan relay and the A/C condenser fan.■
  1. Set the A/C button and fan control button OFF, then turn the ignition switch to LOCK (0).
  1. Reconnect the A/C condenser fan 2P connector.
  1. Connect the A/C condenser fan 2P connector No. 1 terminal to body ground with a jumper wire.
  1. Turn the ignition switch to ON (II), then set the A/C button and fan control button ON.
Does the A/C condenser fan run at high speed?
YES -
Go to Step 23 .
NO -
Replace the A/C condenser fan motor. ■
  1. Set the A/C button and fan control button to OFF, then turn the ignition switch to LOCK (0).
  1. Disconnect the jumper wire.
  1. Disconnect the under-hood fuse/relay box connector C (10P).
  1. Turn the ignition switch to ON (II), then set the A/C button and fan control button ON.
  1. Measure the voltage between under-hood fuse/relay box connector C (10P) No. 10 terminal and body ground.
Is there battery voltage?
YES -
Go to Step 28 .
NO -
Repair open in the wire between the A/C condenser fan relay and the A/C condenser fan.■
  1. Set the A/C button and fan control button to OFF, then turn the ignition switch to LOCK (0).
  1. Remove the relay circuit board from the under-hood fuse/relay box, and test the fan control relay.
Is the relay OK?
YES -
Go to Step 30 .
NO -
Replace the relay circuit board.■
  1. Reinstall the relay circuit board.
  1. Disconnect the radiator fan 2P connector.
  1. Turn the ignition switch to ON (II), then set the A/C button and fan control button ON.
  1. Measure the voltage between the radiator fan 2P connector No. 2 terminal and body ground.
Is there battery voltage?
YES -
Go to Step 34 .
NO -
Repair open in the wire between the fan control relay and the radiator fan.■
  1. Measure the voltage between the radiator fan 2P connector No. 1 and No. 2 terminals.
Is there battery voltage?
YES -
Replace the radiator fan motor. ■
NO -
Check for an open in the wire between the radiator fan 2P connector and body ground. If the wire is OK, check for poor ground at G301. ■
