Cam Chain Installation
NOTE: Keep the cam chain away from magnetic fields.
  1. Set the crankshaft to top dead center (TDC). Align the TDC mark (A) on the crankshaft sprocket with the pointer (B) on the engine block.
  1. Set up the No. 1 piston at TDC. Align the punch mark on the intake camshaft gear in the center of the two punch marks on the exhaust camshaft gear.
  1. Install the cam chain on the crankshaft sprocket with the colored link plate (A) aligned with the punch mark (B) on the crankshaft sprocket.
  1. Install the cam chain on the camshaft sprocket with the punch marks (A) aligned with the two colored link plates (B).
  1. Apply new engine oil to the threads of the cam chain tensioner arm mounting bolt (A) and cam chain guide mounting bolts (B), then install the cam chain tensioner arm (C) and cam chain guide (D).
  1. Install the auto-tensioner.
  1. Remove the stopper or pin from the auto-tensioner.
  1. Check the chain case oil seal for damage. If the oil seal is damaged, replace the chain case oil seal.
  1. Remove all of the old liquid gasket from the chain case mating surfaces, bolts, and bolt holes.
  1. Clean and dry the chain case mating surfaces.
  1. Apply liquid gasket (P/N 08C70-K0234M or 08C70-K0334M) to the engine block mating surface of the chain case and to the inside edge of the bolt holes.
    NOTE: Apply liquid gasket about 2.5 mm (0.098 in.) diameter bead along the broken line (A).
  1. Apply liquid gasket on the engine block upper surface contact areas (B) in about 11 mm (0.43 in.) diameter and about 2.5 mm (0.098 in.) thickness.
  1. Apply liquid gasket on the lower block upper surface contact areas (C) in 10 mm (0.39 in.) x 6.5 mm (0.25 in.) rectangular and about 2.5 mm (0.098 in.) thickness.
  1. Apply liquid gasket (P/N 08C70-K0234M or 08C70-K0334M) to the oil pan mating surface of the chain case.
    NOTE:
    • Apply liquid gasket about 2.5 mm (0.098 in.) diameter bead along the broken line (A).
    • Do not install components if 5 minutes or more have passed after applying the liquid gasket. Instead, remove the old residue and reapply the liquid gasket.
  1. Set the edge of the chain case (A) to the edge of the oil pan (B), then install the chain case on the block (C). Wipe off excess liquid gasket on the oil pan and chain case mating area.
    NOTE:
    • When installing the chain case, do not slide the bottom surface on the oil pan mounting surface.
    • Wait at least 30 minutes before filling the engine with oil.
    • Do not run the engine for at least 3 hours after installing the chain case.
  1. Remove the side engine mount lower bracket.
  1. Install the side engine mount upper bracket (A), then loosely tighten the new bolts and nut (B), and loosely tighten the bolt (C).
  1. Remove the air cleaner housing assembly.
  1. Loosen the transmission mounting bolt and nuts (A).
  1. Raise the vehicle on the lift to full height.
  1. Loosen the lower torque rod mounting bolts (A).
  1. Lower the vehicle on the lift.
  1. Tighten the side engine mount upper bracket mounting bolts and nut.
  1. Tighten the transmission mounting bolt and nuts.
  1. Raise the vehicle on the lift to full height.
  1. Tighten the lower torque rod mounting bolts.
  1. Lower the vehicle on the lift.
  1. Install the upper torque rod (A) and torque rod stiffener (B), then tighten the new torque rod stiffener mounting bolts (C).
  1. Tighten the new upper torque rod mounting bolts in the numbered sequence shown.
  1. Install the cylinder head cover.
  1. Install the air cleaner housing assembly.
  1. Install the crankshaft pulley.
  1. Install the drive belt auto-tensioner.
  1. Install the idler pulley base.
  1. Install the drive belt.
  1. Raise the vehicle on the lift to full height.
  1. Install the splash shield.
  1. Lower the vehicle on the lift.
