Fuel Pump Circuit Troubleshooting
If you suspect a problem with the fuel pump, check that the fuel pump actually runs; when it is on, you will hear some noise if you listen to the fuel fill port with the fuel fill cap removed. The fuel pump should run for 2 seconds when the ignition switch is turned ON (II). If the fuel pump does not make noise, check as follows:
  1. Turn the ignition switch to LOCK (0).
  1. Remove the driver's dashboard lower cover.
  1. Remove PGM-FI main relay 2 (A) from the driver's under-dash fuse/relay box.
  1. Turn the ignition switch to ON (II).
  1. Measure the voltage between PGM-FI main relay 2 4P connector terminal No. 4 and body ground.
Is there battery voltage?
YES -
Go to Step 14 .
NO -
Go to Step 6 .
  1. Turn the ignition switch to LOCK (0).
  1. Remove PGM-FI main relay 1 (A) and ETCS control relay (B) from the under-hood fuse relay box.
  1. Jump the SCS line with the HDS.
  1. Disconnect ECM/PCM connector A (49P).
  1. Disconnect the engine wire harness 23P connector at left side of engine compartment.
  1. Disconnect the driver's under-dash fuse/relay box connector F (33P).
  1. Connect PGM-FI main relay 1 4P connector terminal No. 2 to body ground with a jumper wire.
  1. Check for continuity between driver's under-dash fuse/relay box connector F (33P) terminal No. 31 to body ground.
