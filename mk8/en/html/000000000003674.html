Presupply Fuel Pump Circuit Troubleshooting
If you suspect a problem with the presupply fuel pump, check that the presupply fuel pump actually runs; when it is on, you will hear some noise if you listen to the fuel fill port with the fuel fill cap removed. The presupply fuel pump should run for about 40 seconds when the ignition switch is turned on. If the presupply fuel pump does not make noise, check as follows:
  1. Turn the ignition switch to LOCK (0).
  1. Remove the driver's dashboard lower cover.
  1. Remove PGM-FI main relay 3 (FUEL PUMP) (A) in the driver's under-dash fuse/relay box.
  1. Turn the ignition switch to ON (II).
  1. Measure the voltage between PGM-FI main relay 3 (FUEL PUMP) 4P connector terminal No. 4 and body ground.
Is there battery voltage?
YES -
Go to Step 14
NO -
Go to Step 6 .
  1. Turn the ignition switch to LOCK (0).
  1. Remove PGM-FI main relay 1 (ECU) (A) from the under-hood fuse/relay box.
  1. Turn the ignition switch to LOCK (0), and wait 2 minutes.
  1. Disconnect ECM connector No. 2 (58P).
  1. Disconnect the driver's under-dash fuse/relay box connector F (33P).
  1. Disconnect the engine wire harness 31P connector at left side of engine compartment.
  1. Connect PGM-FI main relay 1 (ECU) 4P connector terminal No. 2 to body ground with a jumper wire.
  1. Check for continuity between driver's under-dash fuse/relay box connector F (33P) terminal No. 31 and body ground.
Is there continuity?
YES -

  • If needed, replace the driver's under-dash fuse/relay box. ■
NO -

  • If the wire is OK, replace the under-hood fuse/relay box. ■
