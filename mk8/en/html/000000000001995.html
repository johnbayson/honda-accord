Engine Oil Pump Overhaul

Exploded View
Oil Pump Removal
  1. Remove the crankshaft pulley.
  1. Set the crankshaft to top dead center (TDC). Align the key (A) on the crankshaft with the pointer (B) on the chain case.
  1. Remove the oil pan.
  1. Loosely install the crankshaft pulley.
  1. To hold the rear balancer shaft, insert a 8 mm long pin punch (A) (Snap-on PPC110LA or equivalent) into the maintenance hole in the balancer shaft holder and through the rear balancer shaft.
  1. Turn the crankshaft counterclockwise to compress the oil pump chain auto-tensioner.
  1. Compress the auto-tensioner fully, then insert the stopper (P/N 14511-RL0-003) or 2.5 mm (0.10 in.) diameter pin. Turn the crankshaft clockwise to secure the pin.
  1. Remove the oil pump chain auto-tensioner.
  1. Loosen the oil pump sprocket mounting bolt.
  1. Remove the oil pump sprocket (A), key (B), and oil pump chain guide mounting bolt (C).
  1. Remove the oil pump.
Oil Pump Inspection
  1. Remove the pump housing.
  1. Align the tip of the inner rotor tooth (A) with the mark (B) on the outer rotor, then check the inner-to-outer rotor radial clearance between the inner rotor and the outer rotor. If the inner-to-outer rotor radial clearance exceeds the service limit, replace the oil pump.
    Inner Rotor-to-Outer Rotor Radial Clearance
    Standard (New):
    0.05−0.17 mm
    (0.0020−0.0067 in.)
    Service Limit:
    0.21 mm (0.0083 in.)

  1. Check the housing-to-rotor axial clearance between the rotor (A) and lower balancer shaft holder (B). If the housing-to-rotor axial clearance exceeds the service limit, replace the oil pump.
    Housing-to-Rotor Axial Clearance
    Standard (New):
    0.020−0.055 mm
    (0.0008−0.0022 in.)
    Service Limit:
    0.1 mm (0.004 in.)

  1. Check the housing-to-outer rotor radial clearance between the outer rotor (A) and lower balancer shaft holder (B). If the housing-to-outer rotor radial clearance exceeds the service limit, replace the oil pump.
    Housing-to-Outer Rotor Radial Clearance
    Standard (New):
    0.15−0.21 mm
    (0.006−0.008 in.)
    Service Limit:
    0.23 mm (0.009 in.)

Balancer Shaft Inspection
  1. Seat the balancer shaft by pushing it away from the oil pump sprocket end of the oil pump.
  1. Zero the dial indicator against the end of the balancer shaft, then push the balancer shaft back and forth and read the end play.
    Balancer Shaft End Play
    Standard (New):
    0.070−0.140 mm
    (0.0028−0.0055 in.)
    Service Limit:
    0.15 mm (0.006 in.)

  1. Remove the pump housing.
  1. Remove the oil strainer.
  1. Remove the upper balancer shaft holder (with bearings) (A), then remove the front balancer shaft (B) and rear balancer shaft (C).
  1. Measure the inner diameter of the No. 1 bearing for the rear balancer shaft hole.
    Bearing Inner Diameter
    Standard (New):
    24.000−24.020 mm
    (0.9449−0.9457 in.)
    Service Limit:
    24.03 mm (0.946 in.)

  1. Measure the diameter of the No. 1 journal on the rear balancer shaft.
    Journal Diameter
    Standard (New):
    23.938−23.950 mm
    (0.9424−0.9429 in.)
    Service Limit:
    23.92 mm (0.942 in.)

  1. Clean both balancer shaft No. 2 journals and balancer shaft holders with a clean shop towel.
  1. Place one strip of plastigage across each No. 2 journals.
  1. Reinstall the bearings and upper balancer shaft holder, then torque the bolts.
    NOTE: Do not rotate the balancer shafts during inspection.
  1. Remove the upper balancer shaft holder and bearings again, and measure the widest part of the plastigage. If the balancer shaft No. 2 journal oil clearance is out-of-tolerance, replace the balancer shafts and recheck. If it still out-of-tolerance; replace the oil pump.
    No. 2 Journal Oil Clearance
    Standard (New):
    0.050−0.082 mm
    (0.0020−0.0032 in.)
    Service Limit:
    0.11 mm (0.004 in.)

  1. Clean both balancer shaft No. 3 journals and bearings with a clean shop towel.
  1. Place one strip of plastigage across each No. 3 journals.
  1. Reinstall the bearings and upper balancer shaft holder, then torque the bolts.
    NOTE: Do not rotate the balancer shafts during inspection.
  1. Remove the upper balancer shaft holder and bearings again, and measure the widest part of the plastigage. If the balancer shaft No. 3 journal oil clearance is out-of-tolerance, install new bearings, and recheck. If it still out-of-tolerance; replace the balancer shafts.
    No. 3 Journal Oil Clearance
    Standard (New):
    0.060−0.120 mm
    (0.0024−0.0047 in.)
    Service Limit:
    0.15 mm (0.006 in.)

  1. Align the punch mark on the rear balancer shaft in the center of the two punch marks on the front balancer shaft, then install the balancer shafts on the lower balancer shaft holder.
  1. Apply new engine oil to the threads of the 10 mm bolts (A).
  1. Install the oil control orifice (B) with new O-ring (C), then install the upper balancer shaft holder (D).
  1. Install the oil strainer (A) with new O-ring (B).
  1. Install the outer rotor (A) and inner rotor (B) in the balancer shaft holder, then install the oil pump housing (C).
Oil Pump Installation
  1. Remove the crankshaft, and check the key (A) on the crankshaft and the pointer (B) on the chain case are aligned.
  1. Align the groove (A) on the rear balancer shaft with the mark (B) on the oil pump.
  1. Pour new engine oil (30 mL (1.01 fl·oz, 1.06 Imp·oz)) into the oil hole (A). To hold the rear balancer shaft, insert a 8 mm long pin punch (B) (Snap-on PPC110LA or equivalent) into the maintenance hole in the balancer shaft holder and through the rear balancer shaft.
  1. Install the oil pump.
  1. Apply new engine oil to the threads of the oil pump sprocket mounting bolt (A).
  1. Install the key (B) and the oil pump sprocket (C), and tighten the mounting bolt.
  1. Apply new engine oil to the threads of the oil pump chain guide mounting bolt (D), then install it.
  1. Remove the pin punch (E).
  1. Install the oil pump chain auto-tensioner.
  1. Remove the stopper from the oil pump chain auto-tensioner.
  1. Install the oil pan.
  1. Install the crankshaft pulley.
