A/T Paddle Shifter - (Downshift Switch) Illumination Circuit Troubleshooting
SRS components are located in this area. Review the SRS component locations and the precautions and procedures in the SRS before doing repair or service.
  1. Remove the driver's airbag.
  1. Disconnect the paddle shifter − (downshift switch) connector.
  1. Turn the ignition switch to ON (II), and turn the combination light switch to the SMALL (PARKING) position.
  1. Measure the voltage between paddle shifter − (downshift switch) connector terminal No. 1 and body ground.
Is there battery voltage?
YES -
Go to Step 8 .
NO -
Go to Step 5 .
  1. Turn the ignition switch to LOCK (0), and turn the combination light switch to the OFF position.
  1. Check the No. 6 (7.5 A) fuse in the passenger's under-dash fuse/relay box.
Is the fuse OK?
YES -
Repair open in the wire between paddle shifter − (downshift switch) connector terminal No. 1 and the passenger's under-dash fuse/relay box.■
NO -
Go to Step 7 .
  1. Check for continuity between paddle shifter − (downshift switch) connector terminal No. 1 and body ground.
Is there continuity?
YES -
Repair short to body ground in the wire between paddle shifter − (downshift switch) connector terminal No. 1 and the passenger's under-dash fuse/relay box.■
NO -
Install a new No. 6 (7.5 A) fuse in the passenger's under-dash fuse/relay box, then go to Step 4 .
  1. Disconnect the gauge control module connector.
  1. Check for continuity between paddle shifter − (downshift switch) connector terminal No. 2 and gauge control module connector terminal No. 2.
Is there continuity?
YES -
Check the illumination control circuit in the gauge control module self-diagnostic function. If the test results are OK, replace the paddle shifter − (downshift switch). ■
NO -
Repair open in the wire between paddle shifter − (downshift switch) connector terminal No. 2 and the gauge control module.■
