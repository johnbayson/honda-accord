Adaptive Cruise Control/LKAS Symptom Troubleshooting - Adaptive cruise control (ACC)/LKAS indicator (orange) does not go off
Adaptive cruise control (ACC)/LKAS indicator (orange) does not go off
  1. Turn the ignition switch OFF.
  1. Connect the HDS to the data link connector.
  1. Check for DTCs with the HDS.
Is any DTC indicated?
YES -
Troubleshoot the indicated DTC.■
NO -
Go to Step 4 .
  1. Clear the DTCs with the HDS.
  1. Start the engine, and wait for 2 seconds.
  1. Watch the adaptive cruise control (ACC)/LKAS indicator.
Does the adaptive cruise control (ACC)/LKAS indicator go off?
YES -
Intermittent failure. The system is OK at this time. Check for loose or poor connections.■
NO -
Go to Step 7 .
  1. Turn the ignition switch OFF.
  1. Turn the ignition switch ON (II).
  1. Check for adaptive cruise control (ACC) system DTCs with the HDS.
Is any DTC indicated?
YES -
Troubleshoot the indicated DTC.■
NO -
Go to Step 10 .
  1. Check for PCM DTCs with the HDS.
Is DTC U0104 indicated?
YES -
Go to Step 11 .
NO -
Go to the gauge control module self-diagnostic function. ■
  1. Check for VSA system DTCs with the HDS.
Is DTC 86 indicated?
YES -
Go to Step 12 .
NO -
Go to the gauge control module self-diagnostic function. ■
  1. Check for adaptive cruise control (ACC) system DTCs with the HDS.
Is DTC 107 indicated?
YES -
Do the millimeter wave radar unit aiming procedure. ■
NO -
Go to the adaptive cruise control (ACC) system troubleshooting (No DTC indication). If OK, replace the adaptive cruise control (ACC) unit. ■
