Adaptive Cruise Control (ACC)/LKAS System Troubleshooting
NOTE
  • Retrieve DTCs, using the HDS.
  • The adaptive cruise control (ACC)/LKAS system consists of the LKAS control unit, adaptive cruise control (ACC), a LKAS camera and the EPS (LKAS) control unit. Depending on the trouble that has been occurred, each unit or the camera individually writes in DTCs. Thus, when a trouble occurs, determine which unit writes these DTCs at first, and then starts diagnosing the trouble from the units or the camera the DTCs are coming from.
  1. Align the front wheels straight ahead.
  1. Turn the ignition switch OFF.
  1. Connect the HDS to the data link connector (DLC) (A).
  1. Turn the ignition switch ON (II).
    NOTE: See the HDS Help menu for specific instructions.
  1. Watch the adaptive cruise control (ACC)/LKAS indicator.
Does the adaptive cruise control (ACC)/LKAS indicator come on?
YES -
Go to Step 6 .
NO -
Go to Step 8 .
  1. Make sure the HDS communicates with the adaptive cruise control (ACC) unit.
Is there communication?
YES -
Go to Step 7 .
NO -
Go to Step 18 .
  1. Make sure the HDS communicates with the LKAS control unit.
Is there communication?
YES -
Go to Step 8 .
NO -
Go to Step 21 .
  1. Check for DTCs in the adaptive cruise control (ACC) unit DTCs MANU with the HDS.
Is DTC 70 indicated?
YES -
Go to Step 9 .
NO -
