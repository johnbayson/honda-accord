DTC P0983:
 Open in Shift Solenoid Valve D Circuit
NOTE:
  • Record all freeze data and review General Troubleshooting Information before you troubleshoot.
  • This code is caused by an electrical circuit problem and cannot be caused by a mechanical problem in the transmission.
  1. Clear the DTC with the HDS.
  1. Start the engine in the P position.
  1. Check that DTC P0983 recurs.
Is DTC P0983 indicated?
YES -
Go to Step 7 .
NO -
Go to Step 4 .
  1. Choose Shift Solenoid D in Miscellaneous Test Menu, and test the shift solenoid valve D with the HDS.
  1. Start the engine in the P position.
  1. Monitor the OBD status for P0983 in the DTCs/Freeze Data in A/T Mode Menu for a pass/fail.
Does the result indicate a fail?
YES -
Go to Step 7 .
NO -
Intermittent failure, the system is OK at this time. Check for poor connections or loose terminals at the shift solenoid valve D and PCM. If the tester indicates NOT COMPLETE, return to Step 4 and recheck.■
  1. Turn the ignition switch OFF.
  1. Jump the SCS line with the HDS.
  1. Disconnect PCM connectors A (31P) and C (22P).
  1. Measure the resistance between PCM connector terminals C5 and A8 or A9.
Is there 12−25 Ω?
YES -
Check for loose or poor connections at PCM connector terminal C5. If the connection is OK, update the PCM if it does not have the latest software, or substitute a known-good PCM, then recheck. If the symptom/indication goes away with a known-good PCM, replace the original PCM .■
NO -
Go to Step 11 .
  1. Disconnect the shift solenoid harness connector at the transmission housing.
