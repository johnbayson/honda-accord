DTC P1297:
 ELD Circuit Low Voltage
  1. Turn the ignition switch ON (II).
  1. Check the ELECTRICAL LOAD DETECTOR in the DATA LIST with the HDS.
Is 72 A or more indicated?
YES -
Go to Step 3 .
NO -
Intermittent failure, system is OK at this time. Check for poor connections or loose terminals at the ELD and the ECM.■
  1. Turn the ignition switch OFF.
  1. Disconnect the ELD 3P connector.
  1. Turn the ignition switch ON (II).
  1. Check the ELECTRICAL LOAD DETECTOR in the DATA LIST with the HDS.
Is 72 A or more indicated?
YES -
Go to Step 7 .
NO -
Go to Step 10 .
  1. Turn the ignition switch OFF, and wait 30 seconds.
  1. Disconnect ECM connector No. 2 (58P).
  1. Check for continuity between ELD 3P connector terminal No. 3 and body ground.
Is there continuity?
YES -
Repair short in the wire between the ELD and ECM connector No. 2 terminal No. 23, then go to Step 12 .
NO -
Go to Step 18 .
  1. Turn the ignition switch OFF.
  1. Replace the under-hood fuse/relay box.
  1. Reconnect all connectors.
  1. Turn the ignition switch ON (II).
  1. Reset the ECM with the HDS.
  1. Start the engine.
  1. Turn on the headlights.
  1. Check for Temporary DTCs or DTCs with the HDS.
Are any Temporary DTCs or DTCs indicated?
YES -
If DTC P1297 is indicated, check for poor connections or loose terminals at the ELD and the ECM, then go to Step 1 . If any other Temporary DTCs or DTCs are indicated, go to the indicated DTC's troubleshooting.
NO -
Troubleshooting is complete.■
